Lecture 1: Endo1_2: Overview of the Endocrine System & Neuroendocrine Systems

The endocrine system (內分泌系統) is a network of glands and cells that release hormones (荷爾蒙) into the bloodstream to regulate metabolism, growth, reproduction, homeostasis, and mood. Understanding this system has driven major advances in human health — from IVF and contraceptive pills to recombinant protein hormones and the ongoing challenge of endocrine-disrupting chemicals. This lecture establishes the chemical nature of hormones, their mechanisms of action, and the critical interface between the nervous and endocrine systems through the hypothalamic-pituitary axis.

1. General Concepts of the Endocrine System

The endocrine system (內分泌系統) coordinates long-range physiological regulation through chemical messengers. Key concepts:

  • Hormone definition (荷爾蒙定義) — secreted in very small amounts by specialized cells or glands into the blood; carried by the bloodstream to distant target tissues; interacts with specific receptors (受體) to produce a biological response.
  • Ernest Starling (1866–1927) — discovered secretin (促胰液素), the first hormone, with William Bayliss in 1902; coined the term "hormone" (from Greek "to arouse or excite") in 1905.
  • Endocrine system regulates: metabolism (代謝), growth and development (生長與發育), homeostasis (體內平衡), reproduction (生殖), and mood.

Three stimuli that regulate hormone secretion (荷爾蒙分泌調控):

  • Humoral changes (體液變化) — e.g., blood Ca²⁺, blood sugar, and osmolarity (滲透壓).
  • Neural stimuli (神經刺激) — e.g., sympathetic (交感神經) control of the adrenal medulla (腎上腺髓質).
  • Hormonal stimuli (荷爾蒙刺激) — e.g., neurohormones of the hypothalamus (下丘腦) controlling anterior pituitary (前垂體) hormone release.

Endocrine system vs. nervous system (神經系統):

  • Hormones act over longer distances and longer time scales — minutes to days; neurotransmitters (神經傳遞物質) act in milliseconds.
  • Close relationship: hypothalamic neurosecretory hormones regulate many endocrine glands; sympathetic system controls adrenal medulla.
  • Same molecule can be both neurotransmitter and hormone — e.g., norepinephrine (正腎上腺素).
  • Fast neurotransmitter system and slow hormone system work in a complementary (互補的) fashion.
Exam trap: The hypothalamus is part of the brain (神經組織), not a classical endocrine gland, yet it secretes neurohormones. Students confuse "neural stimulus" with "the nervous system does not interact with the endocrine system" — the two systems interact extensively.

2. Chemistry of Hormones

Hormones fall into four chemical categories (四大化學類別):

1. Amino Acid Derivatives (胺基酸衍生物)

  • Synthesized from amino acids (胺基酸).

  • Synthesis pathway (合成路徑) from tyrosine: Tyrosine → DOPA → Dopamine (多巴胺) → Norepinephrine → Epinephrine.
  • Key enzymes (關鍵酵素): tyrosine hydroxylase (酪胺酸羥化酶), amino acid decarboxylase (胺基酸脫羧酶), dopamine β-hydroxylase, phenylethanolamine-N-methyltransferase (PNMT) — final step stimulated by cortisol (皮質醇).
  • Sympathetic stimulation drives conversion from tyrosine through dopamine; cortisol drives the final step to epinephrine.

2. Polypeptides (多肽)

  • Range from 3 to hundreds of amino acids in size.
  • Often produced as larger molecular weight precursors (前體) that are proteolytically cleaved (蛋白水解切割) to the active form.
  • Example: insulin (胰島素) — has two polypeptide chains (A chain: 21 amino acids; B chain: 30 amino acids) linked by disulfide bonds (雙硫鍵); first synthesized as preproinsulin, then proinsulin, then active insulin.

3. Steroids (類固醇)

  • Derived from cholesterol (膽固醇) via pregnenolone (孕烯醇酮) as the common precursor.
  • Examples: cortisol (皮質醇), aldosterone (醛固酮), testosterone (睪固酮), estradiol (雌二醇).

4. Prostaglandins (前列腺素)

  • Synthesized in cell membranes from arachidonic acid (花生四烯酸) — a 20-carbon polyunsaturated fatty acid (5,8,11,14-eicosatetraenoic acid) — produced by cleavage of membrane phospholipids (磷脂) by phospholipases (磷脂酶).
  • Found in almost all tissues; act in an endocrine or paracrine (旁分泌) fashion.
  • Lipid-soluble (脂溶性) but bind to surface receptors (表面受體) coupled with cAMP signaling — distinct from other lipid-soluble hormones that act via nuclear receptors.
  • Nine classes: PGA, PGB, PGC, PGD, PGE, PGF, PGG, PGH, PGI.
  • Collectively called eicosanoids (二十碳類脂素) along with related compounds.
  • NSAIDs (非類固醇抗炎藥) — aspirin and ibuprofen derivatives — inhibit prostaglandin formation involved in fever (發燒), pain (疼痛), and inflammation (發炎); also tested for Alzheimer's disease treatment.
Exam trap: Prostaglandins are lipid-soluble but do NOT act via nuclear receptors — they use surface receptors coupled to cAMP, behaving like lipid-insoluble hormones. Glucose derivatives are NOT a hormone category — the answer to "which may NOT be a hormone" is "derivatives of glucose."

3. Mechanisms of Hormone Action

Mechanism depends on solubility (溶解性):

  • Lipid-soluble hormones (脂溶性荷爾蒙) — diffuse through the plasma membrane; bind to receptors in the nucleus (細胞核) or cytoplasm (細胞質); hormone-receptor complex acts directly on DNA to modify transcription (轉錄); produce long-lasting effects (持久效果) lasting hours to days. Includes thyroxine.
  • Lipid-insoluble hormones (水溶性荷爾蒙) — cannot cross membrane; bind to surface receptors (表面受體); effects mediated by second messengers (第二傳訊物質) or intrinsic catalytic activity of receptors; produce rapid, transient responses (短暫效果).
  • Elwood Jensen (1920–2012) — first described the estrogen receptor (雌激素受體) in 1958 and discovered the superfamily of nuclear hormone receptors (核荷爾蒙受體超家族).

Hormones coupled to GPCRs (G蛋白偶聯受體):

  • cAMP signaling — stimulatory: Epinephrine via beta-adrenoreceptors (β-腎上腺受體), TSH, vasopressin in kidney, glucagon (升糖素), serotonin (血清素), PGI.
  • cAMP signaling — inhibitory: Epinephrine via alpha-2 adrenoreceptors (α₂-腎上腺受體).
  • IP₃/DAG signaling: Vasopressin in liver, growth factors, TRH.

Enzyme-coupled receptors (酵素偶聯受體):

  • Receptor tyrosine kinases (受體酪胺酸激酶, RTKs) — bind insulin and growth factors (生長因子); have intrinsic kinase activity; phosphorylate (磷酸化) tyrosine residues on cytoplasmic proteins when activated; also autophosphorylate (自我磷酸化) for positive feedback (正回饋).
  • ANP receptor — is a receptor guanylate cyclase (鳥苷酸環化酶受體); ligand binding triggers formation of cGMP (環磷酸鳥苷).
Exam trap: Insulin is water-soluble, uses a receptor tyrosine kinase (NOT a GPCR), and is first synthesized as a larger precursor (proinsulin). Students commonly assume all peptide hormones use GPCRs. Amino acid derivatives are not all lipid-insoluble — thyroxine is lipid-soluble and acts via nuclear receptors.

4. Neuroendocrine Systems: General Concepts

Neurosecretory cells (神經分泌細胞) are specialized neurons that secrete neurohormones (神經荷爾蒙). Located in two main organs:

  • Hypothalamus (下丘腦):
    • Magnocellular neurons (大細胞神經元) in the supraoptic nucleus (SON, 視上核) and paraventricular nucleus (PVN, 室旁核) — project axons to the posterior pituitary (後垂體).
    • Parvocellular neurons (小細胞神經元) — project to hypophyseal portal capillaries (垂體門脈毛細血管).
  • Adrenal medulla (腎上腺髓質): chromaffin cells (嗜鉻細胞) — same embryological origin (胚胎起源) as neural tissue.

Neurosecretory cells vs. ordinary neurons (普通神經元):

  • Structurally: secretory granules (分泌顆粒) are 100–400 nm, much larger than synaptic vesicles (突觸小泡) at 30–60 nm; terminate in clusters in a bed of capillaries called the neurohemal organ (神經血管器官).
  • Functionally: respond to sensory inputs (感覺輸入) like other neurons but release hormones into blood, forming an interface (界面) between the neural and endocrine systems.
  • Neurosecretory cells are excitable neurons (可興奮神經元) — they fire action potentials (動作電位); they are NOT non-excitable cells.

Hypothalamus and pituitary gland (下丘腦與垂體):

  • Pituitary gland (垂體 / 腦下垂體, hypophysis) — endocrine gland at the base of the brain, connected to the hypothalamus; has anterior and posterior lobes.
  • Anterior lobe (前葉, adenohypophysis) — true endocrine tissue (真正腺體組織), including pars intermedia (中間葉); secretes regular glandular hormones. "Adeno-" = gland (Greek).
  • Posterior lobe (後葉, neurohypophysis) — neural tissue composed of axons of neurosecretory cells from the hypothalamus; secretes neurohormones.

Hypothalamic-Hypophyseal Portal System (下丘腦-垂體門脈系統):

  • Two capillary beds (毛細血管床) directly joined by portal vessels (門脈血管) — allows hypothalamic neurohormones to reach anterior pituitary at high concentration without dilution in general circulation.
  • Updated view (Tissier et al., Nature Review Endocrinology, 2017): tanycytes (中間細胞) ensheath neuron terminals and create a selective barrier (選擇性屏障) and anatomical plasticity (可塑性); pituitary cells organized into intermingled networks; complex inputs modify neurohormone output at the median eminence (正中隆起); changes in blood flow in portal circulation alter exposure of pituitary cells to neurohormones.
Exam trap: The anterior pituitary is true endocrine (glandular) tissue — NOT composed of axons. The posterior pituitary IS composed of axons of hypothalamic neurosecretory cells. Students frequently swap these two. Also: the hypothalamus secretes hormones TO the portal system — it does not receive hormones from the portal system. The hypothalamus is located below the thalamus (not above it).

5. Anterior Pituitary Hormones and Hypothalamic Releasing Hormones

The anterior pituitary secretes two categories of glandular hormones (前垂體腺體荷爾蒙):

  • Direct-acting hormones (直接作用荷爾蒙) — act on somatic non-endocrine target tissues:
    • Growth hormone (GH, 生長激素)
    • Prolactin (PRL, 泌乳素)
    • Melanocyte-stimulating hormone (MSH, 促黑色素細胞激素) — by pars intermedia
  • Tropic hormones (促腺激素) — act on other endocrine tissues:

    • Follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH, 促卵泡激素)
    • Luteinizing hormone (LH, 促黃體激素)
    • Thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH, 促甲狀腺激素)

Prolactin (泌乳素, PRL) details:

  • Named for its effect on milk protein synthesis (乳蛋白合成) and mammary gland (乳腺) growth; has many additional effects beyond lactation.
  • Produced not only by pituitary but also by uterus (子宮), breasts, immune cells (免疫細胞), and prostate (前列腺).
  • Stimulated by TRH and estrogen (雌激素); inhibited by dopamine (多巴胺) — also known as prolactin inhibiting hormone (PIH, 泌乳素抑制激素).

Melanocyte-stimulating hormone (MSH, 促黑色素細胞激素) details:

  • Acts on melanocytes (黑色素細胞) in skin to produce more melanin (黑色素), causing skin darkening (皮膚色素沉著).
  • Generated by different cleavages of proopiomelanocortin (POMC, 前阿黑皮素原) — same precursor that yields ACTH.
  • Also produced by keratinocytes (角質細胞) in skin in response to UV light (紫外線).
  • Increased by estrogen during pregnancy and by other causes.
  • Causes of high MSH: (1) Hypothalamic level — Addison's disease (愛迪生病) → low cortisol from adrenal cortex → no negative feedback on CRH → high CRH → high MSH; (2) Pituitary level — pregnancy → estrogen/progesterone → high MSH; also birth control pills; (3) Receptor level — mutation in MSH receptors; TB (tuberculosis, 肺結核) damaging adrenal gland.
  • Melasma (肝斑) — dark discolored patches on skin; more common in women; called "mask of pregnancy" when occurring during pregnancy; causes include high estrogen and sun exposure.

Hypothalamic releasing and release-inhibiting hormones (下丘腦釋放與抑制激素):

  • Secreted by parvocellular neurons; transported to anterior pituitary via the hypothalamic-hypophyseal portal system.
  • Either stimulate or inhibit release of anterior pituitary hormones.
  • Mnemonic: All direct and tropic hormones have releasing hormones. Only direct hormones (GH, prolactin, MSH) have release-inhibiting hormones (simplified rule). MSH and ACTH arise from the same precursor (POMC).

Structure and evolution (結構與演化) of ADH and oxytocin:

  • Both are 9-amino acid peptides (九肽) with a disulfide bond (雙硫鍵).
  • ADH and oxytocin sequences differ at positions 3 and 8 — closely related paralogs derived from a common ancestral peptide.
  • Vasopressin family: Argipressin/AVP (most mammals), Lypressin/LVP (pigs, hippos, warthogs), Phenypressin (some marsupials), Vasotocin (non-mammals).
  • Oxytocin family: Oxytocin (most mammals), Mesotocin (most marsupials, all reptiles, amphibians, lungfishes), Seritocin (frogs), Isotocin (bony fishes), Glumitocin (skates), various tocins (sharks).
Exam trap: Oxytocin stimulates milk ejection (射乳), NOT milk synthesis (乳汁合成) — prolactin stimulates milk protein synthesis. ADH acts via cAMP/adenylate cyclase, not a nuclear receptor, because it is a peptide (water-soluble). Growth hormone and prolactin are anterior pituitary hormones, not neurohormones — ADH (vasopressin) is the neurohormone.

Top things to memorize
  1. Four chemical categories of hormones: amino acid derivatives, polypeptides, steroids, prostaglandins. Glucose derivatives are NOT one.
  2. Lipid-soluble hormones → nuclear receptors → long-lasting effects (hrs–days). Exception: prostaglandins are lipid-soluble but use surface cAMP receptors.
  3. Lipid-insoluble hormones → surface receptors (GPCRs or RTKs) → second messengers → rapid, transient effects.
  4. Insulin: polypeptide, water-soluble, receptor tyrosine kinase, synthesized as precursor (proinsulin), two chains (A-21aa, B-30aa).
  5. Anterior pituitary: direct hormones (GH, PRL, MSH) and tropic hormones (ACTH, FSH, LH, TSH). Only direct hormones have release-inhibiting hormones from hypothalamus.
  6. MSH and ACTH both come from POMC; CRH drives both. Addison's disease → high CRH → hyperpigmentation via MSH.
  7. Posterior pituitary neurohormones: ADH (water retention via AQP2 in kidney collecting duct; blood pressure via vascular smooth muscle) and oxytocin (milk ejection; uterine contractions).
  8. Hypothalamic-hypophyseal portal system: two capillary beds joined by portal vessels; parvocellular neurons release hypothalamic hormones here to reach anterior pituitary.
  9. Magnocellular neurons (SON, PVN) → posterior pituitary. Parvocellular neurons → portal system → anterior pituitary.
  10. Neurosecretory cell granules: 100–400 nm vs. synaptic vesicles: 30–60 nm.
  11. TRH stimulates both TSH and prolactin. Dopamine (PIH) inhibits prolactin. Somatostatin inhibits GH, TSH, insulin, and glucagon.
  12. GLP-1: gut hormone acting on GPCR; enhances insulin secretion, inhibits glucagon, slows gastric emptying, promotes satiety; key drug target for type 2 diabetes and obesity.

Lecture 2: Endo3_4: Metabolic & Developmental Hormones + Water/Electrolyte Balance

This lecture covers the six major metabolic and developmental hormones — epinephrine/norepinephrine, glucocorticoids, thyroid hormones, insulin/glucagon, growth hormone, and leptin — then pivots to the three hormones that govern water and electrolyte balance: ADH, aldosterone, and ANP. Together these systems coordinate the body's energy economy under stress, regulate growth across the lifespan, and maintain blood volume and osmolality. The throughline is homeostatic balance: every hormone introduced has a counterpart or feedback axis that prevents runaway effects.

1. Epinephrine & Norepinephrine

  • Catecholamines (兒茶酚胺) are secreted by the adrenal medulla (腎上腺髓質) — the inner, neural (ectodermal) layer of the adrenal gland; the outer cortex (腎上腺皮質) is mesodermal.
  • Epinephrine (腎上腺素) constitutes ~80% of secretion; norepinephrine (去甲腎上腺素) is the precursor for epinephrine synthesis via phenylethanolamine-N-methyltransferase (PNMT), which is stimulated by cortisol.
  • Two types of chromaffin cells (嗜鉻細胞) — one produces epinephrine, the other norepinephrine; chromaffin cells are neurosecretory and analogous to postganglionic neurons (節後神經元) of the sympathetic nervous system (交感神經系統).
  • Preganglionic sympathetic neurons release acetylcholine (乙醯膽鹼) onto chromaffin cells, triggering catecholamine release into the bloodstream.

  • The adrenal medulla functions as an extension of the sympathetic nervous system; activated by cold, stress, hemorrhage, and exercise — same stimuli as the sympathetic system.
  • Net effect: reinforces and prolongs the sympathetic response, increasing the body's capacity for vigorous muscle activity.

System / Tissue Effect of Epinephrine/Norepinephrine
Cardiovascular (心血管) Increased cardiac output, heart rate, and strength of contraction; vasodilation in skeletal muscle; vasoconstriction in internal organs and skin
Smooth muscle (平滑肌) Relaxation of GI tract, urinary bladder, and airway smooth muscle
CNS (中樞神經系統) Increased mental alertness
Metabolism (代謝) Increased glycogenolysis (糖原分解) in liver and muscle; increased lipolysis (脂肪分解) in adipose tissue; decreased insulin secretion; increased glucagon secretion

  • Adrenoreceptors (腎上腺素受體) include alpha and beta subtypes, each coupled to different second-messenger pathways: alpha via phospholipase C (磷脂酶C) → IP3 + DAG; beta via adenylate cyclase (腺苷酸環化酶) → cAMP.
  • Key pharmacological agents: phenylephrine (alpha1-agonist), clonidine (alpha2-agonist), phentolamine (mixed alpha-antagonist), isoproterenol (beta-agonist), salbutamol (beta2-agonist), practolol (beta1-antagonist), butoxamine (beta2-antagonist).
  • Pheochromocytoma (嗜鉻細胞瘤) — adrenal medulla tumor causing excessive adrenaline release; symptoms: periodic hypertension (高血壓), palpitations, sweating, headaches, anxiety; may lead to persistently high blood pressure.
  • Epinephrine is the first-line treatment for anaphylaxis (過敏性休克): vasoconstrictor effects prevent airway mucosal edema (黏膜水腫) and shock; bronchodilator (支氣管擴張劑) and cardiac inotropic/chronotropic (強心/變時) effects are also important.

Exam trap: The adrenal medulla secretes catecholamines only — NOT glucocorticoids or mineralocorticoids. Glucocorticoids come from the outer cortex. A common false statement is "the medulla secretes glucocorticoids and catecholamines" — this is FALSE.
Exam trap: Epinephrine DECREASES insulin secretion and INCREASES glucagon secretion. Do not confuse with insulin's own effects. A statement that "epinephrine increases insulin secretion" is FALSE.

2. Glucocorticoids

  • Glucocorticoids (糖皮質激素) are steroid hormones (固醇激素) secreted by the adrenal cortex (腎上腺皮質).
  • Major forms: cortisol (皮質醇 / hydrocortisone, 氫化可的松) — dominant form in humans; also cortisone (可的松) and corticosterone (皮質酮).
  • Regulation axis: stress or hypoglycemia → hypothalamic CRH (促腎上腺皮質素釋放激素) → anterior pituitary ACTH (促腎上腺皮質素) → adrenal cortex releases glucocorticoids → negative feedback (負反饋) on hypothalamus and pituitary.
  • Diurnal rhythm (晝夜節律): plasma cortisol peaks in the morning and is lowest around midnight.

  • Primary effect: provoke gluconeogenesis (糖質新生) — formation of glucose from non-carbohydrate molecules (fats and proteins) under stress; critical for brain energy supply during starvation.
  • Liver: increases gluconeogenesis, protein degradation (蛋白質降解), and decreases glucose uptake.
  • Skeletal muscle: decreases protein synthesis and glucose uptake.
  • Adipose tissue: decreases glucose uptake; stimulates lipid mobilization (脂質動員) and free fatty acid release.
  • Enhance vascular reactivity (血管反應性).
  • Suppress inflammation (抗炎) and the immune system (免疫系統) at higher doses — used clinically as immunosuppressants (免疫抑制劑) in transplantation and as lympholytics (淋巴細胞溶解劑) in chemotherapy.

  • Cushing's syndrome (庫欣氏症候群): caused by excessive cortisol in the blood, resulting from glucocorticoid drugs, or from tumors producing cortisol, ACTH, or CRH.
  • Major symptoms: rapid weight gain, moodiness and depression, muscle weakness, immune suppression, capillary dilation (毛細血管擴張), skin thinning, and baldness.

Exam trap: Cortisol is also called hydrocortisone — these are the same compound. The major glucocorticoid in humans is cortisol (B/C in quiz). Corticosterone is a different, minor form. Do not select corticosterone as the "major" glucocorticoid.

3. Thyroid Hormones

  • Thyroid hormones (甲狀腺激素) are formed from two iodinated (碘化) tyrosine precursors in the thyroid gland (甲狀腺).
  • Thyroxine (甲狀腺素, T4 = tetraiodothyronine, 四碘甲狀腺原氨酸) is the major released form (2 tyrosine + 4 iodine); triiodothyronine (三碘甲狀腺原氨酸, T3) is more potent and is converted from T4 in target tissues (2 tyrosine + 3 iodine).
  • Goiter (甲狀腺腫) results from dietary iodide deficiency, preventing adequate thyroid hormone synthesis.
  • Receptors are nuclear (核受體) and act as transcriptional factors (轉錄因子); they also have non-genomic actions (非基因組作用) that do not directly affect gene expression.
  • Regulation axis: CNS/temperature-regulating center → hypothalamic TRH (促甲狀腺素釋放激素) → anterior pituitary TSH (促甲狀腺素) → thyroid releases T3/T4 → negative feedback on hypothalamus and pituitary. TSH also stimulates growth of the thyroid gland.
  • Stimuli: prolonged cold; increased energy need (e.g. pregnancy).

  • Stimulate cellular respiration (細胞呼吸) — oxidation of organic substances — to increase basal metabolic rate (基礎代謝率) and heat production; acts on all tissues EXCEPT brain, spleen, testes/uterus, and the thyroid itself.
  • Sensitize tissues to epinephrine (使組織對腎上腺素敏感) — reason why hyperthyroidism causes increased cardiac rate and strength of contraction.
  • Required for normal development and maturation (發育成熟) of skeletal and nervous systems; this action also requires the presence of growth hormone.

Exam trap: Hyperthyroidism causing increased cardiac contraction rate is due to sensitization of tissues to epinephrine — NOT direct stimulation of catecholamine secretion from the adrenal medulla. The mechanism is sensitization, not increased catecholamine release.

4. Insulin, Glucagon & Diabetes

  • Insulin (胰島素) and glucagon (升糖素) are peptide hormones (肽激素) secreted by islets of Langerhans (蘭氏小島) in the pancreas (胰臟) — insulin from beta cells (β細胞, 51 amino acids, 2 chains), glucagon from alpha cells (α細胞, 29 amino acids, single chain).
  • "Insu-" derives from Latin insula = island; the two hormones have opposite effects on blood glucose (血糖).

  • Humoral stimuli for insulin: elevated blood glucose (major stimulus); elevated blood amino acids and fatty acids. Insulin is the "hormone of nutrient abundance."
  • Hormonal stimuli: gastric inhibitory peptide (GIP, 抑胃肽) signals meal ingestion; glucagon, epinephrine, glucocorticoids, and GH have direct or indirect stimulatory effects (via increasing blood glucose).
  • Neural control: sympathetic activation inhibits (抑制) insulin release; parasympathetic activation stimulates (促進) insulin release.

  • Carbohydrate metabolism: increases uptake and storage of glucose as glycogen (糖原) in liver, muscle, and adipose tissue; suppresses glycogenolysis.
  • Fat metabolism: stimulates lipogenesis (脂肪合成) in liver and adipose tissue.
  • Protein metabolism: stimulates uptake of amino acids in liver and muscle; incorporates amino acids into proteins.
  • Kidney tubules: increases absorption of glucose from filtrate.
  • Insulin acts through second-messenger system (第二信使系統) — it is a peptide hormone, not a steroid receptor-mediated hormone.

  • Glucagon secretion: mainly humoral — inhibited by elevated blood glucose and fatty acids (regulation by blood glucose requires presence of insulin).
  • Stimulated by elevated blood amino acids (same as insulin); glucagon then promotes conversion of amino acids to glucose.
  • Glucagon effects: (1) glycogenolysis (糖原分解) in liver → glucose release into blood; (2) increases gluconeogenesis in the liver.

  • Diabetes mellitus (糖尿病): defect in the insulin signaling system; name from Greek "siphon" (excessive thirst and urination) + Latin mellitus = honey (sweet urine). Distinct from diabetes insipidus (尿崩症) which is ADH deficiency.
  • Type 1 diabetes (第一型糖尿病, insulin-dependent, 10% of cases): loss of pancreatic beta cells → decreased insulin production.
  • Type 2 diabetes (第二型糖尿病, insulin-independent, 90% of cases): defective insulin receptors.
  • Gestational diabetes (妊娠糖尿病): the third form, occurring in pregnant women.
  • Epidemiology: 7th leading cause of death globally; ~14% adults worldwide as of 2022; 10–12% adults in Hong Kong; ~4.2 million deaths from complications in 2019 (1 life per 8 seconds); global economic cost estimated at US$1.3 trillion in 2024.

  • Acute complications: hyperglycemia (高血糖) → glycosuria (糖尿, spillover of glucose into urine) → increased urine volume/frequency → dehydration.
  • Type 1 acute: absence of insulin removes inhibition on glucagon → high glucagon → increased ketone body (酮體) formation in liver → ketoacidosis (酮酸中毒) → electrolyte imbalance → dehydration → coma/death.
  • Chronic vascular complications: narrowing of large vessels in brain, heart, lower extremities → stroke, heart attack, limb loss; lesions in small vessels/capillaries in kidney and retina → nephropathy (腎病) and retinopathy (視網膜病變) / blindness; fatty liver disease.
  • Neural complications: damaged autonomic nerves → bladder/GI dysfunction, impotence; damaged peripheral sensory nerves → loss of feeling in lower limbs (diabetic foot, 糖尿病足); peripheral neuropathy (周圍神經病變); bone disease.

Exam trap: Insulin is secreted by BETA cells, glucagon by ALPHA cells — not the other way around. A common false statement: "both are secreted by alpha cells in islets of Langerhans" — this is FALSE. Also, insulin acts through second-messenger pathways, not as a steroid hormone with a nuclear receptor.
Exam trap: In Type 1 diabetes, glucagon levels are HIGH (not low) because insulin normally inhibits glucagon secretion — absence of insulin removes that brake. A question option "low glucagon level" is NOT associated with Type 1 diabetes mellitus.

5. Growth Hormone & Leptin

  • Growth hormone (生長激素, GH) is a peptide hormone (191 amino acids in humans) produced by the anterior pituitary (腦垂體前葉).
  • Overall effect: promote tissue growth — regarded as an anabolic hormone (合成代謝激素).
  • Secreted in a phasic manner (間歇性); peaks approximately 1 hour after sleep onset; pulses occur 2–4 hours after a meal; pulse frequency and size increase at puberty and decline with aging.

  • Stimulators of GH secretion: low blood glucose (hypoglycemia, 低血糖); amino acids, especially arginine (精氨酸); deep sleep; stress — physical trauma, infection, psychological stress.
  • Inhibitors: high blood glucose concentration; REM (rapid eye movement) sleep.
  • Regulation axis: hypothalamus secretes GHRH (stimulates) and somatostatin/GIH (生長抑素, inhibits) → anterior pituitary GH → liver releases somatomedins/IGFs (胰島素樣生長因子).

  • GH has both insulin-like (類胰島素) and anti-insulin effects.
  • Direct actions on tissues: increase muscle mass → accumulation of lean body mass (瘦體重); decrease fat storage; inhibit glucose uptake and utilization in most tissues (anti-insulin); stimulate gluconeogenesis in liver; stimulate fatty acid mobilization in fat cells; stimulate amino acid uptake and protein synthesis in muscle.
  • Indirect actions via IGFs/somatomedins (生長介素): linear growth of the skeleton; growth of many organs and tissues.
  • Three mechanisms of hyperglycemic effect of GH: (1) lipolysis — lipids replace glucose as energy source; (2) direct inhibition of glucose uptake in tissues; (3) increased gluconeogenesis in liver.
  • GH stimulates insulin secretion by: (1) direct action on pancreatic beta cells; (2) indirect effect via its hyperglycemic action.

  • GH deficiency: pituitary defect or defect in IGF-I production/response. Childhood deficiency → dwarfism (侏儒症); adult deficiency (after normal bone growth) → no evident effect.
  • Laron dwarfs (拉隆侏儒): genetic mutation inhibiting GH receptor production; notably protected from cancer and diabetes.
  • GH excess in childhood → gigantism (巨人症); in adulthood → acromegaly (肢端肥大症) — enlarged jaw, nose, and hands.

Exam trap: Most of GH's growth-promoting metabolic effects on liver, muscle, and adipose tissue are mediated by IGF-1 (not IGF-2, not T4). Dreaming (REM sleep) inhibits GH secretion; arginine stimulates it; high blood glucose inhibits it.

  • Leptin (瘦素, from Greek leptos = "thin") is a 16 kDa peptide hormone encoded by the obese gene (ob gene, 肥胖基因), primarily produced by white adipose tissue (白色脂肪組織) but also by many other tissues.
  • Function: influences food intake relative to energy expenditure — when leptin levels are high, appetite is reduced and energy expenditure is increased.
  • Leptin receptor (LepR / OB-R, 瘦素受體) is expressed primarily in the brain; most effects on end organs are indirect via the CNS. Leptin also regulates immune-cell function via CNS and directly.
  • LepR is a cytokine family receptor (細胞因子受體) coupled to tyrosine kinases (JAKs, 酪氨酸激酶).
  • Possible reasons for elevated leptin in obese individuals: (1) higher body fat percentage → higher absolute leptin production; (2) partial leptin resistance (瘦素抵抗) at its site of action; (3) perceived state of starvation (感知飢餓).
  • Leptin also influences growth, adrenal glucocorticoid function, glycaemic control (血糖控制), and reproduction via CNS pathways.

6. Water & Electrolyte Balance: ADH, Aldosterone & ANP

  • Antidiuretic hormone (抗利尿激素, ADH), also called vasopressin (血管加壓素), is a cyclic nonapeptide (環形九肽, 9 amino acids); synthesized in the supraoptic nucleus (視上核) of the hypothalamus and secreted by the posterior pituitary (腦垂體後葉).
  • Actions: (1) increases water reabsorption (水重吸收) in the kidney by increasing surface expression of aquaporins (水通道蛋白); (2) regulates blood pressure by direct action on vascular smooth muscle (血管平滑肌) — hence "vasopressin."
  • Alcohol inhibits ADH secretion, explaining the diuretic effect of alcohol intake (increased urine output).

  • Osmotic regulation (滲透壓調節): osmoreceptors (滲透壓感受器) in the anterior hypothalamus (possibly the supraoptic neurons themselves) sense plasma osmolality (血漿滲透壓); a 5–10 mOsm change triggers ADH secretion (normal ~280 mOsm); elevated osmolality → ↑ ADH → ↑ water reabsorption → reduced urine volume.
  • Volume/blood pressure regulation: greater than 10% blood volume decrease (e.g. excessive bleeding) activates stretch receptors (牽張感受器, TRP channels) in cardiovascular system neurons; signal transmitted to pituitary via cranial nerves (腦神經) → ↑ ADH → vasoconstriction + ↑ water reabsorption → ↑ blood volume and pressure.

Exam trap: Drinking a lot of water will NOT stimulate ADH secretion — it dilutes plasma osmolality, which SUPPRESSES ADH. Salty food, hemorrhage, and increased plasma osmolality all stimulate ADH; drinking water suppresses it.

  • Mineralocorticoids (鹽皮質激素) are steroids secreted by the adrenal cortex that regulate kidney water/electrolyte balance; major form is aldosterone (醛固酮).
  • Action of aldosterone: stimulates Na+ reabsorption (鈉離子重吸收) and K+ excretion (鉀離子排泄) with H2O following Na+; net effect is increased extracellular fluid volume (細胞外液容量) → increased blood volume and blood pressure.
  • Humoral regulation: extracellular K+ concentration — a 5% change can trigger aldosterone secretion.
  • Hormonal regulation: (a) Angiotensin II (血管緊張素II) — primary hormonal factor regulating aldosterone secretion, stimulated by blood pressure decrease; (b) ACTH (促腎上腺皮質素).
  • Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS, 腎素-血管緊張素-醛固酮系統): decreased renal perfusion → juxtaglomerular apparatus (旁腎小球裝置) releases renin (腎素) → liver angiotensinogen → angiotensin I → ACE in lung endothelium → angiotensin II → adrenal cortex secretes aldosterone → tubular Na+/Cl- reabsorption and K+ excretion → water/salt retention → increased circulating volume. Angiotensin II also causes arteriolar vasoconstriction → increased blood pressure.

  • Atrial natriuretic peptide (心房利鈉肽, ANP) is secreted by endocrine cells in the atria (心房) of the heart in response to mechanical stretch (機械牽張), e.g. blood volume increase.
  • Acts on kidneys: increases Na+ excretion and urine production — natriuresis (利鈉尿); "natriuretic" from natrium (sodium) + urine.
  • ANP counteracts (拮抗) the effects of both ADH and aldosterone — promotes sodium and water loss when blood volume is too high.

Exam Traps Summary
  • The adrenal medulla secretes catecholamines ONLY — not glucocorticoids. "Medulla secretes glucocorticoids and catecholamines" is FALSE.
  • Epinephrine DECREASES insulin secretion and INCREASES glucagon secretion — not the reverse.
  • Cortisol = hydrocortisone; both names refer to the same major glucocorticoid. Corticosterone is a separate, minor form.
  • Hyperthyroidism causing increased cardiac contraction rate is due to sensitization of tissues to epinephrine — NOT stimulation of catecholamine secretion from the adrenal medulla.
  • Insulin is from BETA cells; glucagon is from ALPHA cells. "Both secreted by alpha cells" is FALSE.
  • Insulin is a peptide hormone acting via second-messenger pathways — not a steroid-receptor mechanism.
  • In Type 1 diabetes, glucagon is HIGH (not low) because insulin normally inhibits glucagon secretion; loss of insulin removes that inhibition.
  • Most GH growth-promoting metabolic effects are mediated by IGF-1 (somatomedins) — not IGF-2, not T4.
  • GH is stimulated by hypoglycemia, arginine, and deep sleep; inhibited by high blood glucose and REM sleep. Dreaming (REM) inhibits GH.
  • Drinking a lot of water SUPPRESSES ADH (lowers plasma osmolality). Only salty food, hemorrhage, and high osmolality stimulate ADH.
  • ANP OPPOSES ADH and aldosterone — it increases Na+ excretion and lowers blood pressure, while ADH and aldosterone increase Na+/H2O retention and raise blood pressure. They do not all work on the same target tissue.
Top things to memorize
  1. Adrenal medulla (inner, neural, ectodermal) = catecholamines only; adrenal cortex (outer, mesodermal) = glucocorticoids + mineralocorticoids + androgens.
  2. Glucocorticoid axis: stress/hypoglycemia → CRH (hypothalamus) → ACTH (anterior pituitary) → cortisol (adrenal cortex) → negative feedback. Cortisol = hydrocortisone.
  3. Cortisol primary action: gluconeogenesis from fats/proteins under stress; also anti-inflammatory/immunosuppressive at high doses; diurnal peak in the morning.
  4. Thyroid hormones: T4 = major released form (2 Tyr + 4 I); T3 = more potent, converted from T4 in tissues. Nuclear receptors act as transcription factors. Goiter from iodide deficiency. Thyroid hormones sensitize tissues to epinephrine; required for skeletal and nervous system maturation with GH.
  5. Insulin from beta cells (51 aa, 2 chains); glucagon from alpha cells (29 aa, single chain). Insulin = storage (glycogen, lipogenesis, protein synthesis); glucagon = mobilization (glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis). Amino acids stimulate BOTH; glucose stimulates insulin and inhibits glucagon.
  6. Type 1 diabetes (10%): beta-cell loss → no insulin → high glucagon → ketoacidosis. Type 2 (90%): defective insulin receptors. Gestational = 3rd form.
  7. GH = anterior pituitary, 191 aa, anabolic. Stimulated by hypoglycemia, arginine, deep sleep, stress. Inhibited by high glucose, REM sleep. Peaks ~1 h after sleep onset.
  8. GH direct: increase lean body mass, decrease fat, anti-insulin effects. GH indirect: via IGF-1 → linear skeletal growth, organ growth. GH also causes hyperglycemia via lipolysis, decreased glucose uptake, and increased gluconeogenesis.
  9. Leptin (ob gene, 16 kDa, from adipose tissue): high leptin = reduced appetite + increased energy expenditure. LepR mainly in brain (indirect CNS effects). Obese individuals often leptin-resistant despite high leptin levels.
  10. ADH (vasopressin): 9 aa, posterior pituitary (synthesized in hypothalamus). Increases aquaporin expression → water reabsorption; also vasoconstricts. Regulated by osmolality (5–10 mOsm change at 280 mOsm normal) and blood volume (stretch receptors via cranial nerves).
  11. Aldosterone (adrenal cortex): stimulates Na+ reabsorption + K+ excretion + H2O retention → increased blood volume/pressure. Regulated by K+ concentration, angiotensin II (primary), and ACTH. RAAS is the key regulatory cascade.
  12. ANP (cardiac atria): released by stretch (blood volume increase); increases Na+ excretion (natriuresis) → reduces blood volume and pressure; antagonizes ADH and aldosterone.

Lecture 3: Endo5 — Reproductive Hormones

Reproductive hormones coordinate the development of sex characteristics, the production of gametes, and the cyclical preparation of the uterus for pregnancy. The system operates through a shared steroid biosynthesis pathway — cholesterol to progesterone to androgens to estrogens — overlaid with peptide signals from the pituitary. Understanding the feedback logic (positive in the follicular phase, negative in the luteal phase) and the distinct roles of cell types such as Leydig cells and Sertoli cells in the male, and follicle and corpus luteum in the female, is the central throughline of this lecture.

1. Overview of Sex Hormones and Reproductive Organs

Primary vs. secondary sex characteristics (初級與次級性特徵):

  • Primary sex characteristics (初級性特徵) — directly related to reproduction; include the genitalia (生殖器官)
  • Secondary sex characteristics (次級性特徵) — other distinguishing attributes: voice quality, facial hair abundance, breast development

Reproductive organ classes:

  • Gonads (性腺) = primary sex organs: ovary (卵巢) and testis (睪丸)
    • Produce gametes (配子)
    • Produce sex hormones (性激素) that regulate reproduction and development/function of secondary sex organs
  • Secondary sex organs (次級性器官) — e.g. uterus (子宮) and penis (陰莖); functions: transport, protection, and nourishment of gametes, embryo, or young

Sex hormone classes and biosynthetic pathway (生合成途徑):

  • All primary sex hormones are steroids (類固醇) derived from cholesterol (膽固醇)
  • Biosynthetic sequence: Cholesterol → Progesterone (黃體素) → Androgens (雄性激素) → Estrogens (雌激素)
  • Androgens — mainly testosterone (睪固酮); more important in males
  • Estrogens — mainly 17β-estradiol (雌二醇); more important in females
  • Progesterone — more important in females
  • Two additional peptide hormones (胜肽激素) from the pituitary (腦下垂體): oxytocin (催產素) and prolactin (泌乳素); function in childbirth and lactation (哺乳)

Exam trap: Not all reproductive hormones are steroids — oxytocin (nonapeptide) and prolactin (peptide) are produced in the pituitary, not the gonads. Also, androgens are present in both males and females (adrenal cortex produces them in females); they are not exclusive to males.

2. Male Reproductive System and Spermatogenesis

Male sex organs and associated structures:

  • External organs: penis (陰莖) — copulatory organ and common exit for urinary and reproductive systems; scrotum (陰囊) — houses testes outside abdominal cavity because spermatogenesis (精子生成) cannot occur at normal body temperature
  • Two testes (睪丸) — spermatogenesis occurs in seminiferous tubules (精曲小管); Leydig cells (萊迪希細胞) make testosterone
  • Epididymis (副睪) — site of final sperm maturation; takes several weeks
  • Vas deferens (輸精管) — sperm transport through inguinal canal to abdominal cavity and out urethra
  • Seminal vesicle (精囊) — adds ~60% of fluid to semen; contains fructose (果糖, energy source), prostaglandins (前列腺素), proteins to coagulate (使凝固) semen in vagina
  • Prostate gland (前列腺) — adds seminal fluids that neutralize the acidic urethra and vagina

Leydig cells (萊迪希細胞) — also called interstitial cells (間質細胞):

  • Located outside the seminiferous tubules in the interstitial space (間質空間)
  • Produce testosterone (睪固酮)
  • Respond to LH (黃體化激素) stimulation

Spermatogenesis (精子生成):

  • Process by which germ cells (生殖細胞) undergo a series of divisions (分裂) and differentiations (分化) to become mature sperm
  • Takes place in the seminiferous tubules; "seminiferous" = seed-producing (semin- = seed, -ferous = producing)
  • Two cell types in seminiferous tubules:
    1. Germ cells — making sperm; include spermatogonia (精原細胞)
    2. Sertoli cells (塞托利細胞) — supporting cells
  • Average college-age male produces ~60 million sperm/mL semen; fertility markedly impaired if sperm count <35 million/mL
  • Note: sperm counts may be declining globally due to environmental pollutants (環境污染物), per systematic review by Levine et al. (2017); methodological issues preclude definitive conclusion

Sertoli cell (塞托利細胞) functions:

  • Form blood-testes barrier (血睪屏障)
  • Produce seminiferous tubular fluid (精曲小管液) that stimulates and nourishes developing germ cells
  • Synthesize inhibin (抑制素) — a peptide hormone that inhibits FSH release

Exam trap: Sertoli cells bind FSH, not LH — LH targets Leydig cells. Sertoli cells secrete inhibin; they do NOT secrete testosterone. Inhibin specifically inhibits FSH release, not LH.

Neuroendocrine control of spermatogenesis (神經內分泌調控):

  • GnRH (促性腺激素釋放激素) from hypothalamus (下視丘) triggers release of LH and FSH from anterior pituitary (前腦下垂體)
  • Both LH and FSH are required for spermatogenesis
  • LH stimulates testosterone synthesis in Leydig cells
  • Testosterone exerts negative feedback (負回饋) on GnRH, LH, and FSH release
  • FSH binds receptors on Sertoli cells and stimulates spermatogenesis (also requires testosterone)
  • Inhibin from Sertoli cells provides negative feedback specifically on FSH release
  • Sensory stimuli — visual, olfactory (嗅覺), or tactile (觸覺); thoughts and moods — can regulate GnRH secretion
  • Stress, e.g. protracted aerobic exercise (持續有氧運動), inhibits GnRH secretion in both males and females: inhibits sperm production in males; disrupts menstruation (月經) in females

Actions of testosterone (睪固酮的作用):

  • Prenatal development (產前發育) — triggers development of primary male sex characteristics
  • Puberty (青春期) — enlargement of male sex organs (penis, scrotum, testes) and development of secondary sex characteristics
  • Spermatogenesis — required in seminiferous tubules
  • General growth and protein synthesis (蛋白質合成) — especially muscle proteins (肌肉蛋白), giving greater muscle mass in males
  • Behavioral changes — aggression (攻擊性)

3. Female Reproductive System and Oogenesis

Female sex organs:

  • Vulva (外陰部) — external genitalia; includes clitoris (陰蒂), labia minora (小陰唇), labia majora (大陰唇)
  • Vagina (陰道) — muscular canal for reception of penis and passage of infant at birth
  • Uterus (子宮) — muscular organ that holds and nourishes the developing fetus; endometrium (子宮內膜) is the inner secretory tissue layer made of glands and connective tissue; cervix (子宮頸) extends into vagina
  • Uterine tube / oviduct / fallopian tube (輸卵管) — site of fertilization (受精) of ovum; transports ovum from ovary to uterus
  • Two ovaries (卵巢) — produce ova (卵子) and female sex hormones (primarily estrogens and progesterone); all production occurs within the follicles (卵泡)
  • Female system has separate exits for urinary and reproductive systems (unlike male)

Oogenesis (卵子生成):

  • Process of formation, development, and maturation of ova (ovum 卵子, plural ova)
  • Midway through each ovulatory cycle (~28 days), a single ovum is expelled by one ovary into the uterus
  • ~400–500 mature ova produced over a woman's reproductive lifespan (puberty to menopause 停經)
  • Ova formed in primary follicles (初級卵泡) in the ovary; follicle = oocyte (卵母細胞) + supporting cells (支持細胞)
  • Supporting cells provide nutrients (營養), chemical signals, and a barrier
  • After ovulation (排卵), the follicle becomes the corpus luteum (黃體)
  • Corpus luteum degenerates if no fertilization and implantation (著床) occur

4. Reproductive Cycle — Ovarian and Uterine

Overview:

  • Purpose: produce gametes and prepare uterus for implantation
  • Average ~28 days in humans; consists of two parallel cycles: ovarian cycle (卵巢週期) and uterine cycle (子宮週期)

Ovarian cycle (卵巢週期) phases:

  • Follicular (preovulatory) phase (卵泡期) — growth and development of follicles; lasts 10–14 days
  • Ovulation (排卵) — release of egg around day 14
  • Luteal phase (黃體期) — transformation of the follicle into the corpus luteum; occupies the remainder of the cycle

Uterine cycle (子宮週期) phases:

  • Menses (月經) — menstrual bleeding; sloughing off of endometrium lasting ~5 days; triggered by corpus luteum degeneration causing estrogen and progesterone to fall
  • Proliferative phase (增生期) — endometrium adds a new functional layer of cells
  • Secretory phase (分泌期) — corpus luteum hormones enhance endometrium secretions

Detailed hormonal events across the cycle:

  • Days 0–10 (early follicular): Previous corpus luteum disintegrates (no implantation) → negative feedback on hypothalamus is removed → GnRH increases → FSH and LH rise → follicle stimulated by FSH; begins to grow and produce estrogen
  • Days 10–12: Rising estrogen from growing follicle provides positive feedback (正回饋) to hypothalamus → increased hypothalamic activation
  • Days 12–14: Increased estrogen leads to buildup of endometrium; increased hypothalamic activation produces LH and FSH surge (波峰) from anterior pituitary → LH/FSH surge triggers ovulation on ~day 14
  • Days 14–28 (luteal phase): Follicle transforms to corpus luteum; corpus luteum secretes progesterone and some estrogen; high progesterone/estrogen provides negative feedback on hypothalamus → decreased FSH and LH; progesterone/estrogen also stimulate endometrium buildup; inhibin (released from follicle at ovulation) also inhibits FSH release
  • Late luteal (no implantation): Corpus luteum degenerates → progesterone and estrogen fall → menstruation triggered → next cycle begins

Exam trap: Estrogen has opposite feedback effects depending on cycle phase — it exerts positive feedback on GnRH/LH/FSH during the follicular phase (driving the LH surge and ovulation) but negative feedback during the luteal phase (suppressing further FSH and LH). Ovulation is triggered BY the LH surge; at that point follicular estrogen is not preventing ovulation — it caused the surge.

Pregnancy and chorionic gonadotropin (絨毛膜促性腺激素, hCG):

  • After ovulation, ovum travels down fallopian tubes; fertilization usually occurs in fallopian tubes, several days after ovulation
  • Fertilized ovum implants in the endometrium
  • Within a day after implantation, placenta (胎盤) begins to secrete chorionic gonadotropin (CG / hCG)
  • hCG "rescues" the corpus luteum, preventing it from degenerating
  • Corpus luteum maintains progesterone and estrogen production for the first trimester (頭三個月)
  • After the first trimester, placenta itself secretes progesterone and estrogen

5. Female Reproductive Hormones — Effects and Clinical Applications

Effects of estrogen (雌激素的作用):

  • Stimulates growth of primary sex organs (first sex characteristics)
  • Stimulates development of secondary sex characteristics
  • Stimulates growth of long bones (長骨) and early closure of epiphyses (骨骺板閉合) — limits final height
  • Stimulates GnRH, LH, and FSH release during the follicular phase (positive feedback); inhibits release during the luteal phase (negative feedback)
  • Decreases sensitivity (敏感性) of peripheral tissues to insulin (胰島素) — clinically relevant in pregnancy
  • Protects against bone loss (骨質流失) in women

Gestational diabetes (妊娠糖尿病):

  • Caused by decreased sensitivity of peripheral tissues to insulin during pregnancy
  • Usually develops at ~20–24 weeks of pregnancy
  • As placenta grows larger, more estrogen is produced → greater insulin resistance (胰島素阻抗)
  • Most women: pancreas makes additional insulin to compensate; gestational diabetes results when maximal pancreatic insulin output is still insufficient to overcome placental hormone effect

Effects of progesterone (黃體素的作用):

  • Stimulates full development of the endometrium (子宮內膜) — prepares it for pregnancy
  • Stimulates further development of mammary glands (乳腺)
  • Provides negative feedback to hypothalamus to prevent further ovulation — prevents ovulation during pregnancy
  • Antagonizes (拮抗) effects of testosterone and dihydrotestosterone (二氫睪固酮) — prevents target tissues in females from responding to androgens

Contraceptive pill (避孕藥):

  • Contains estrogen + progestogen (合成黃體素)
  • Effectiveness: 99.7%
  • Prevents ovulation by suppressing release of gonadotropins (促性腺激素)
  • Also inhibits sperm mobility (精子活動力) by increasing viscosity (黏稠度) of cervical mucus (子宮頸黏液)
  • Very safe; most common side effect: "breakthrough bleeding" (突破性出血)
  • First generation pill approved by FDA in 1960; rapid spread generated enormous social impact

In vitro fertilization (體外受精, IVF):

  • Invented by Robert Edwards (Nobel Prize 2010) and Patrick Steptoe; first "test-tube baby" Louise Brown born 1978
  • Over 8 million IVF babies born since 1978
  • IVF routinely requires ovarian hyperstimulation (卵巢過度刺激) to generate multiple eggs
  • Induction of oocyte maturation requires human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG 人絨毛膜促性腺激素)
  • Unwanted embryos (胚胎) sometimes used in stem cell research (幹細胞研究), which is ethically controversial

Exam trap: Gestational diabetes is caused by high estrogen producing insulin resistance — not by excessive carbohydrate ingestion, high progesterone, increased GI glucose absorption, or excessive glucagon. Estrogen is the direct cause via decreased peripheral insulin sensitivity.
Exam Traps Summary
  • Not all reproductive hormones are steroids — oxytocin (nonapeptide) and prolactin (peptide) are pituitary-derived; androgens are also present in females via the adrenal cortex.
  • Sertoli cells bind FSH, not LH; LH targets Leydig cells. Sertoli cells secrete inhibin, not testosterone.
  • Inhibin from Sertoli cells inhibits FSH specifically; testosterone provides negative feedback on GnRH, LH, and FSH.
  • Estrogen has opposite feedback effects by cycle phase: positive feedback in the follicular phase (drives LH surge and ovulation) and negative feedback in the luteal phase.
  • Ovulation is triggered by the LH surge, not prevented by estrogen at that point — follicular estrogen caused the surge.
  • Gestational diabetes is caused by estrogen-induced insulin resistance, not excessive carbohydrate intake, progesterone, increased GI glucose absorption, or glucagon.
  • The oviduct / fallopian tube is the site of fertilization; implantation occurs in the endometrium of the uterus; ova are produced in the ovary.
  • Ovulation can be prevented by high combined estrogen + progesterone (as in the contraceptive pill or luteal phase); FSH, LH, or GnRH alone do not prevent ovulation.
Top things to memorize
  1. Steroid biosynthesis order: Cholesterol → Progesterone → Androgens → Estrogens
  2. Leydig cells (stimulated by LH) → testosterone; Sertoli cells (stimulated by FSH) → seminiferous tubular fluid + inhibin
  3. Both LH and FSH required for spermatogenesis; testosterone provides negative feedback on GnRH/LH/FSH; inhibin provides negative feedback specifically on FSH
  4. Sertoli cell functions: blood-testes barrier, nourish germ cells, secrete inhibin (not LH-responsive, does not secrete testosterone)
  5. Fertility impaired if sperm count <35 million/mL; scrotum is external because spermatogenesis requires below-body temperature
  6. Corpus luteum secretes progesterone and estrogen; degenerates without implantation; "rescued" by hCG from placenta after implantation
  7. LH surge on ~day 14 triggers ovulation; surge is driven by positive feedback from rising follicular estrogen
  8. Progesterone prevents further ovulation during pregnancy via negative feedback on hypothalamus; also antagonizes androgens in females
  9. Estrogen decreases peripheral insulin sensitivity → basis of gestational diabetes developing at ~20–24 weeks
  10. Contraceptive pill (estrogen + progestogen): suppresses gonadotropins to prevent ovulation; increases cervical mucus viscosity; 99.7% effective; FDA approved 1960
  11. Fallopian tube = site of fertilization; endometrium = site of implantation; ovary = site of ova production
  12. Oxytocin and prolactin are peptide hormones from the pituitary, not steroids; oxytocin promotes uterine contraction and milk ejection; prolactin increases milk protein synthesis

Lecture 4: GI1 — Overview of the Digestive System

The digestive system converts food into molecules the body can absorb — ingesting, digesting, absorbing, and excreting in a tightly coordinated sequence from mouth to anus. Understanding the four-segment plan (headgut, foregut, midgut, hindgut) and the layered wall structure explains why each region does what it does. Motility is not simple muscle movement but a regulated behaviour — peristalsis propels, segmentation mixes — both governed by the enteric nervous system, the "little brain in the gut."

1. General Concepts — Types of Digestion & Nutrient Classes

The digestive system (消化系統) consists of the alimentary canal (消化道) plus accessory digestive organs (附屬消化器官), working together for ingestion (攝食), digestion (消化), and absorption (吸收).

Two types of digestion (消化):

  • Mechanical digestion (機械性消化) — solid food masses are torn apart, ground up, and vigorously mixed with digestive secretions (消化液) to prepare food for chemical digestion. Examples: chewing (咀嚼), mixing by tongue, churning (攪拌) in stomach, segmentation (分節, rhythmic local constrictions of intestine).
  • Chemical digestion (化學性消化) — complex food molecules are broken down to their chemical building blocks by enzymes (酶) secreted by various digestive glands.

Three classes of nutrients (營養素) and their fate:

3. Microscopic Structure of the GI Wall

Four concentric layers from lumen outward:

  • Mucosa (黏膜) — innermost; mucous membrane with epithelial (上皮), connective (結締), and smooth muscle (平滑肌) tissues; contains glands and mucosa-associated lymphoid tissue (MALT)
  • Submucosa (黏膜下層) — loose connective tissue with glands; contains the submucosal plexus (Meissner's plexus 梅氏神經叢) of the enteric nervous system — controls secretion
  • Muscularis (肌層) — longitudinal (縱行) and circular (環行) smooth muscle fibres; the myenteric plexus (Auerbach's plexus 奧氏神經叢) lies between these two layers — controls motility
  • Serosa (漿膜) — outermost; visceral peritoneum (腹膜)
Exam trap: The myenteric (Auerbach's) plexus lies between the longitudinal and circular muscle layers — not in the submucosa. The submucosal (Meissner's) plexus is in the submucosa.

4. Types of GI Motility — Peristalsis & Segmentation

Three functions of GI movement (運動): (1) translocate food from point A to B, (2) mechanical breakdown of food, (3) mix food for continual renewal of material in contact with secreting and absorbing surfaces. Food moves down the GI tract even in the absence of gravity — GI mobility requires muscle involvement.

5. Control of Motility — Enteric Nervous System & Innervation

Two levels of control (控制):

  • Intrinsic control (內在控制) — mediated by the enteric nervous system (ENS 腸道神經系統); modulated by stretching of the GI tract and substances in the chyme (食糜, partly digested food in the stomach)
  • Extrinsic control (外在控制): (a) GI peptide hormones (胜肽激素) secreted by the GI tract in response to chemical stimulants; (b) sympathetic (交感神經, inhibitory 抑制) and parasympathetic (副交感神經, excitatory 興奮) nervous systems

The ENS — nicknamed the "little brain in the gut" — contains ~100 million neurons (more than a mouse brain), organized as an independent integrative system with two plexuses:

  • Myenteric plexus (肌層神經叢 / Auerbach's plexus) — between muscle layers; controls motility
  • Submucosal plexus (黏膜下神經叢 / Meissner's plexus) — in submucosa; controls secretion and absorption

ENS neuron types: sensory neurons (感覺神經元) — mechanoreceptors (機械感受器), chemoreceptors (化學感受器), thermoreceptors (溫度感受器); interneurons (中間神經元) — reflexes, motor programs; motor neurons (運動神經元) — inhibitory & excitatory → targets: muscle, secretory epithelium (分泌上皮), endocrine cells, blood vessels.

Extrinsic innervation (外在神經支配):

  • Sympathetic (交感): preganglionic cholinergic (膽鹼能) → postganglionic adrenergic (腎上腺素能) / cholinergic → inhibits GI motility and secretion
  • Parasympathetic (副交感): vagus nerve (迷走神經) — preganglionic cholinergic → postganglionic cholinergic/adrenergic → excites GI motility and secretion

Hirschsprung's disease (先天性巨結腸症): in children, nerves fail to form in all or part of the large intestine; waste cannot pass through the colon lacking nerve tissue; the normal colon swells with blocked stool.

Exam trap: Sympathetic = inhibitory for GI; parasympathetic = excitatory. "Fight or flight" shuts the gut down; "rest and digest" activates it. Many students mistakenly flip these.
Exam Traps Summary
  • Stomach has no absorption — absorption begins in the small intestine (midgut).
  • Segmentation uses circular muscle only — longitudinal muscle is NOT involved (peristalsis uses both).
  • Myenteric (Auerbach's) plexus is between muscle layers; submucosal (Meissner's) plexus is in the submucosa — do not swap them.
  • Sympathetic is inhibitory for GI motility; parasympathetic is excitatory ("rest and digest").
Top things to memorize
  1. Two digestion types: mechanical (tears/grinds/mixes) vs. chemical (enzymes break bonds)
  2. Three nutrients and end-products: protein → amino acids; carbs → monosaccharides (glucose/fructose/galactose); fats → glycerol + fatty acids
  3. Four GI segments: headgut (mouth/pharynx) → foregut (esophagus/stomach) → midgut (small intestine/pancreas/liver) → hindgut (colon/rectum/anus)
  4. Four wall layers (lumen outward): mucosa → submucosa → muscularis → serosa
  5. Two plexuses: myenteric (between muscle layers, motility) vs. submucosal (in submucosa, secretion/absorption)
  6. Peristalsis: both muscles, propulsion, aboral direction; Segmentation: circular muscle only, mixing, no net propulsion
  7. ENS = ~100 million neurons; sensory + interneurons + motor; operates independently
  8. Sympathetic inhibits GI; parasympathetic excites GI
  9. Hirschsprung's disease = absent enteric neurons in colon → stool blockage + colon swelling

Lecture 5: GI2 — Digestion: Mouth, Esophagus, Stomach, Pancreas & Liver

GI Lecture 2 traces the path of food through each digestive organ from mouth to duodenum, covering the mechanical and chemical processes at each site. Every structure — from salivary amylase to the parietal cell's proton pump — is precisely specialized, and understanding their secretions and regulation explains why clinical conditions such as GERD, H. pylori ulcers, and gallstones arise exactly where they do.

1. Mouth & Salivary Glands

The mouth (口腔 / buccal cavity 頰腔) contains three main structures:

  • Teeth (牙齒) — biting, tearing, grinding (mechanical digestion)
  • Tongue (舌頭) — tasting and manipulating food bolus (食團); involved in swallowing
  • Salivary glands (唾液腺) — three pairs: parotid (腮腺), sublingual (舌下腺), submandibular (頜下腺); secrete multifunctional saliva (~1.5 L/day)

Two cell types in salivary glands:

Mucous cells (黏液腺細胞)Mucins (黏蛋白) / glycoproteins (糖蛋白) → mix with serous → viscous mucus (saliva)Mucins

Three functions of saliva (唾液):

  • A. Moistening & lubrication (潤滑) — essential for swallowing and taste
  • B. Protect teeth against dental caries (蛀牙): contains enzyme proteins (peroxidase 過氧化物酶, lysozyme 溶菌酶) and antibacterial proteins (defensins 防禦素); saliva is slightly alkaline (弱鹼性) — neutralizes acids from mouth bacteria and acid refluxed from stomach
  • C. Amylase — begins starch digestion: starch → maltose (麥芽糖, 2 glucose), maltotriose (麥芽三糖, 3 glucose), oligosaccharides (寡醣, >3 glucose)

Defensins (防禦素): secreted by immune cells and epithelial cells; amphipathic (兩親性) and cationic (陽離子); mechanism: disrupt pathogen membrane (破壞病原體細胞膜), block viral receptors and signaling.

Regulation of saliva: salivary centers in brain stem receive two types of stimuli: (A) touch (pressoreceptors 壓力感受器), smell, and taste (taste buds 味蕾); (B) psychological stimuli (e.g. thought of food = conditioned reflex 條件反射); fear inhibits; sleep, fatigue (疲勞), and dehydration (脫水) also inhibit (→ dry mouth). Salivary glands controlled by autonomic nerves (自主神經):

  • Parasympathetic (facial & glossopharyngeal nerves) — promotes moderate amount of watery saliva; keeps mouth, lips, tongue moist
  • Sympathetic — also stimulates secretion but less than parasympathetic; saliva is richer in mucins (mucin-rich 富黏蛋白), more viscous; sympathetic stimulus → dry mouth sensation

Magnitudes of GI secretion (L/day): Saliva 1.5 | Gastric juice 1–3 | Pancreatic juice 2 | Bile 0.6–1.2 | Intestinal secretions 2 | Total 7–10 L/day. Water lost in feces: ~100 ml/day (the rest is reabsorbed).

Exam trap: Sympathetic stimulation of salivary glands still produces saliva, but it is more viscous and mucin-rich — not absent. The subjective sensation is dry mouth because of less total fluid.

2. Pharynx, Esophagus & Swallowing

The pharynx (咽) has three parts: nasopharynx (鼻咽, air only), oropharynx (口咽), laryngopharynx (喉咽). It is a common passageway for food, fluids, and air; the external muscle layer contains two skeletal muscle layers (pharyngeal constrictor muscles 咽縮肌) involved in the swallowing reflex.

Swallowing reflex (吞嚥反射 / deglutition 吞嚥) — sequential steps:

  1. Tongue moves bolus to rear of mouth → presence of object activates swallowing reflex in pharynx
  2. Soft palate (軟顎) closes nasal passage; epiglottis (會厭) moves over glottis (聲門) to close larynx (喉) and trachea (氣管)
  3. Upper esophageal sphincter (上食道括約肌) relaxes → food bolus propelled into esophagus → sphincter re-contracts

The esophagus (食道) is a collapsible muscular tube (~10 inches / ~25 cm) posterior to the trachea; conducts food to stomach by peristalsis (蠕動). Key features:

  • Begins at inferior end of laryngopharynx; passes through diaphragm (橫膈膜) via esophageal hiatus (食道裂孔)
  • Mucosa = stratified squamous epithelium (多層扁平上皮) / smooth muscle (平滑肌)
  • Upper esophageal sphincter (上食道括約肌) — controls passage from laryngopharynx into esophagus
  • Lower esophageal (cardiac) sphincter (下食道括約肌) — controls passage from esophagus into stomach

GERD (胃食道逆流病): failure of lower esophageal sphincter to close after food enters stomach → acid backflow → "heartburn" (胃灼熱) sensation. Affects ~10–20% of Western populations. Risk factors: obesity (肥胖), pregnancy (懷孕), smoking (吸煙).

Exam trap: The epiglottis covers the glottis (trachea opening) — not the esophagus — during swallowing. The esophageal sphincter relaxes to allow food entry. These two events happen simultaneously but are mechanically distinct.

3. Stomach — Structure & Gastric Juice

The stomach (胃) is a J-shaped enlargement of the GI tract connecting the esophagus to the duodenum (十二指腸). Four functions: (1) food reservoir (食物儲存庫), (2) mechanical fragmentation (機械性分解), (3) mix food with gastric secretion → chyme (食糜), (4) empty gastric contents into small intestine at a controlled rate.

Gross structure (大體結構):

  • Cardia (賁門, "near the heart") — entry from esophagus
  • Fundus (胃底)
  • Body (胃體) — midportion
  • Pyloric region (幽門區, funnel-shaped): pyloric antrum (幽門竇 "cave"), pyloric canal (幽門管), pylorus (幽門) with pyloric sphincter (幽門括約肌) that controls stomach emptying

Rugae (胃皺褶): large folds in the mucosa of the empty stomach; enable expansion (膨脹) and distension (伸展).

Five cell types in gastric glands (胃腺) — collectively produce gastric juice (胃液) at 1–3 L/day:

Gastric juice — five components:

  • 1. Mucus — viscous (黏稠), slippery; chiefly mucin, water, inorganic salts. Protective barrier against mechanical and chemical damage. Contains HCO₃⁻ (from surface mucous cells) maintaining a HCO₃⁻ gradient — lumen pH ~2 but pH at cell surface ~7.
  • 2. Pepsinogen → pepsin (胃蛋白酶) — converted at pH < 5; cleaves proteins (蛋白質) → small peptides; maximal activity at pH ~2; pepsin can autocatalyze its own formation from pepsinogen.
  • 3. Gastric lipase — initiates fat digestion: TG → diglyceride + fatty acid; accounts for 10–30% of triglyceride digestion.
  • 4. Intrinsic factor (內因子) — glycoprotein (糖蛋白) that binds vitamin B12; essential for B12 absorption in the ileum (迴腸); needed for RBC production (紅血球生成).
  • 5. HCl — denatures proteins, breaks down food by strong acid; kills ingested microbes; converts pepsinogen to pepsin.

Parietal cell HCl secretion mechanism (壁細胞分泌機制) — three transport processes:

  • 1. H⁺/K⁺ ATPase (proton pump 質子幫浦) — apical; pumps H⁺ into lumen, K⁺ into cell (active, ATP-driven)
  • 2. Cl⁻/HCO₃⁻ exchanger (anion exchanger 陰離子交換蛋白) — basolateral; HCO₃⁻ exits to blood, Cl⁻ enters cell
  • 3. Cl⁻ channel — apical; Cl⁻ exits into lumen alongside H⁺ → HCl formed in lumen
  • Source of H⁺: CO₂ + H₂O → H₂CO₃ → HCO₃⁻ + H⁺, catalyzed by carbonic anhydrase (碳酸酐酶, CA)
Exam trap: Pepsinogen requires pH < 5 for conversion to pepsin, but pepsin's optimal activity is at pH ~2. Do not confuse conversion pH with optimal activity pH.

  • Gastric phase (胃期) — food enters stomach; stretch (distension 膨脹) and chemoreceptors (amino acid/peptide)
  • Intestinal phase (腸期) — food enters intestine; amino acids stimulate acid secretion via circulation; distension stimulatory

Cimetidine (西咪替丁): H₂ histamine receptor blocker (組胺H₂受體阻斷劑) on parietal cells; one of most popular medicines worldwide; prevents heartburn in overproduction of gastric acid.

Helicobacter pylori (幽門螺旋桿菌, H. pylori): bacterium that survives the harsh stomach environment; uses urease (尿素酶) to convert urea → NH₃ + CO₂, neutralizing gastric acid around itself; penetrates mucus layer; ~50% of world population affected; significantly increases risk of gastric ulcer (胃潰瘍).

Exam trap: The three stimuli (histamine, ACh, gastrin) are synergistic — their combined effect is greater than the sum of individual effects. Blocking just one (e.g. histamine via cimetidine) can substantially reduce total acid output.

5. Pancreas & Liver/Bile

The pancreas (胰腺) is a soft oblong gland ~6 in. long / 1 in. thick; posterior to the greater curvature of the stomach; connected to the duodenum by pancreatic (duct of Wirsung 胰管) and accessory ducts (duct of Santorini) via the sphincter of Oddi (奧迪括約肌).

Dual function: endocrine (內分泌, ~1% of gland = islets of Langerhans 蘭格漢斯島: α/β/δ cells for glucagon/insulin/somatostatin) and exocrine (外分泌, 99% = acinar cells + duct cells).

Exocrine pancreas — two secretory cell types:

  • Duct epithelial cells (導管上皮細胞) — secrete large volume watery solution rich in NaHCO₃; neutralizes gastric acid entering duodenum; provides alkaline pH optimal for enzyme activity; flushes sticky protein secretions from acinar cells
  • Acinar cells (腺泡細胞) — secrete small volume of digestive enzymes
  • Combined = pancreatic juice (胰液) at 1–2 L/day

The liver (肝臟) is the largest gland in the body; 2 main lobes (葉) made of lobules (肝小葉). Multifunctional: controls overall body metabolism (非消化功能); bile formation (膽汁形成) is the major exocrine/digestive function.

Secretion of bile (膽汁分泌): hepatocytes (肝細胞) secrete ~1 L bile/day → bile canaliculi (膽小管) → bile ducts → hepatic ducts. Bile flow pathway:

  • During a meal: gallbladder contracts → bile through common bile duct (總膽管) + relaxed sphincter of Oddi → duodenum

Composition of bile (alkaline solution 鹼性溶液): H₂O (97%), NaCl/NaHCO₃, bile salts (膽鹽), cholesterol (膽固醇), phospholipids (磷脂), bile pigments (膽色素).

  • Bile salts: amphipathic (兩親性), made from cholesterol in liver. (A) Emulsify fats (乳化脂肪) in SI — break large lipid globules → smaller droplets accessible to pancreatic lipase. (B) Solubilize cholesterol/phospholipids in bile → prevent gallstones (膽結石).
  • Bile pigments: bilirubin (膽紅素) = breakdown product of hemoglobin (血紅素) → gives bile yellow-green color. High blood bilirubin → jaundice (黃疸). Bile is both an excretory product (bile pigment) and a digestive secretion (bile salts).

Gallstones (膽結石, cholesterol stones): possible causes — enzyme defect, decreased secretion of bile salts, or increased cholesterol synthesis → cholesterol precipitates.

Exam trap: Enteropeptidase (腸激酶) is an intestinal brush border enzyme — not a pancreatic enzyme. It initiates the activation cascade by converting trypsinogen → trypsin. Without enteropeptidase, no pancreatic zymogens are activated.
Exam Traps Summary

  • Epiglottis covers the glottis (trachea opening) — not the esophagus — during swallowing.
  • Pepsinogen activates at pH < 5; pepsin has optimal activity at pH ~2 — these are two different pH thresholds.
  • Three gastric acid stimuli (histamine, ACh, gastrin) are synergistic — blocking one still reduces total acid substantially.
  • Enteropeptidase is an intestinal enzyme, not a pancreatic enzyme — it initiates the zymogen activation cascade.

  • Gallbladder stores and concentrates bile — it does not produce bile (hepatocytes produce bile).

Lecture 6: GI3 — Absorption: Small Intestine & Large Intestine

GI Lecture 3 focuses on absorption — the transfer of digested nutrients from the intestinal lumen into the bloodstream or lymph. The small intestine is extraordinarily specialized for this, amplifying its surface area ~600-fold through circular folds, villi, and microvilli. Each nutrient class uses a distinct molecular strategy: sugars ride a sodium gradient, amino acids and dipeptides use active transporters, and lipids are packaged into micelles before being reassembled as chylomicrons for lymphatic export.

1. Small Intestine — Structure & General Absorption

Absorption (吸收) is the process by which molecules are transported through the epithelial cells (上皮細胞) lining the GI tract → enter the blood (血液) or lymph (淋巴). Key principles:

  • Occurs only after complete chemical digestion — ingested molecules must be cleaved into smaller units first
  • Nutrients (carbohydrates, fats, proteins), water, ions, and some vitamins absorbed primarily in the small intestine (小腸)
  • Absorption of fluid and certain ions also occurs in the large intestine (大腸)

Small intestine (SI): ~6 m long, ~4 cm diameter; accounts for ¾ of GI tract length; chyme takes 2–4 hours to pass through. Three segments:

SegmentLengthKey events
Duodenum (十二指腸)26 cm (5% of SI)Gastric acid neutralized by pancreatic NaHCO₃; digestion by pancreatic enzymes + bile; absorption starts at lower part; secretion of H₂O, electrolytes, mucus
Jejunum (空腸)2.5 m (40%)Main site of absorption; carbohydrate absorption complete here
Ileum (迴腸)3.5 mMore absorption; joins large intestine; B12 absorbed here (with intrinsic factor)

Surface area amplification (表面積放大) — three structural levels:

Villi (絨毛)0.5–1.0 mm, finger-like projections; covered with simple columnar epithelium×10
Microvilli (微絨毛)On absorptive cells (brush border 刷狀緣)×20

Net result: gross surface 0.33 m² (7–8 book pages) → true surface ~200 m² (a doubles tennis court).

Absorption route: nutrients pass through membrane + cytoplasm of absorptive cells → blood and lymph vessel capillaries. Intestinal blood collects → portal vein (門靜脈) → liver (first pass, 肝臟首過效應) → hepatic vein → venous circulation. Lymph vessels eventually drain to venous circulation.

Intestinal transport capacity (mmol/day): Water 1,000,000 (18 L/day) | Glucose 20,000 (3.6 kg/day) | Amino acids 5,000 | Triglycerides 900 | Cholesterol 10 | Iron 0.2 | Vitamin B12 0.000001

Exam trap: Microvilli amplify surface area ×20, villi ×10, circular folds ×3 — students often confuse the order. Remember: smaller structure = bigger relative amplification factor.

2. Carbohydrate Absorption

Dietary carbohydrates (碳水化合物) in two categories:

  • Digestible (可消化的): Major = plant starch (植物澱粉, a polysaccharide 多醣; e.g. rice, bread). Minor = disaccharides (雙醣): sucrose (蔗糖 = 1 glucose + 1 fructose), lactose (乳糖 = 1 glucose + 1 galactose).
  • Indigestible (不可消化的): cellulose (纖維素) = dietary fiber (膳食纖維); excreted in feces.

Digestion pathway: starch → maltose (麥芽糖), maltotriose (麥芽三糖), oligosaccharides (寡醣) by salivary + pancreatic amylases. Only monosaccharides (單醣) can be absorbed — brush border enzymes complete hydrolysis (水解):

Transport mechanisms across enterocytes (腸上皮細胞):

  • Fructose (果糖) — absorbed passively (被動吸收)
  • Glucose & galactose — secondary active transport (次級主動運輸) via SGLT1 (sodium-glucose transporter 1 鈉葡萄糖共同運輸蛋白); downhill Na⁺ flux (順梯度) energizes uphill glucose/galactose entry; Na⁺ then pumped out basolaterally by Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase; glucose/galactose exit across basolateral membrane via GLUT2 (passive)
  • Carbohydrate digestion and absorption complete by the end of the jejunum

Oral Rehydration Therapy (ORT 口服補液療法): prevents and treats dehydration (脫水) from diarrhea; formulation: NaCl + sodium citrate + KCl + glucose. Mechanism: glucose increases salt uptake through SGLT1 → water follows salt absorption (osmosis 滲透作用). Discovered by Robert Crane. ORT reduced risk of death from diarrhea by 93%; on WHO Essential Medicine list.

Lactose intolerance (乳糖不耐症): lack of lactase; undigested lactose remains in intestine → (a) osmotic fluid accumulation (causing distension 脹氣, fullness), (b) bacteria in large intestine ferment lactose → lactic acid (乳酸), CO₂, H₂, methane (甲烷) → cramps (腹痛), nausea (噁心), acid reflux; diarrhea from increased intestinal motility and osmotic effect of lactic acid and undigested lactose.

Exam trap: ORT works because of SGLT1 — glucose is essential to drive Na⁺ (and therefore water) absorption. A solution of water + salt alone without glucose is far less effective because it cannot use SGLT1-mediated cotransport.

Proteins (蛋白質) are mainly digested by pancreatic proteases (胰蛋白酶類); brush border of SI has peptidases (肽酶) that cleave oligopeptides (寡肽) into amino acids (AA 氨基酸), dipeptides (二肽), and tripeptides (三肽).

4. Lipid Absorption

Dietary lipids: triglycerides (三酸甘油酯, TG) account for ~90%; the rest = cholesterol esters (膽固醇酯), phospholipids (磷脂), fat-soluble vitamins (脂溶性維生素 A, D, E, K).

Digestion: TG emulsified (乳化) by bile salts → digested by gastric lipase + pancreatic lipase → free fatty acids (游離脂肪酸, FFA) + monoglycerides (單酸甘油酯, MG).

Mixed micelles (混合微胞): FFA + MG (and other lipid-soluble molecules) form mixed micelles with amphipathic bile salts (兩親性膽鹽). Micelles keep hydrophobic lipids in aqueous solution. The molecules in mixed micelles are in dynamic equilibrium (動態平衡) with single free molecules — micelles serve as a reservoir, replenishing free molecules as they are absorbed at the brush border.

Two routes depending on fatty acid chain length:

  • Short-chain fatty acids (<12 carbons) — absorbed directly into portal vein (門靜脈) → liver
  • Long-chain fatty acids + monoglycerides (>12 carbons) — passively diffuse into absorptive cells → resynthesized in ER (內質網) into TG → packaged in Golgi (高基氏體) with other lipids + apoproteins into chylomicrons (乳糜微粒, 100–500 nm lipoprotein particles) → expelled by exocytosis (胞吐) into central lacteal (中央乳糜管) — too large for capillaries → lymph → venous circulation

Large intestine (大腸/結腸): primarily absorbs water (水) and ions (離子) and some vitamins; accumulates and dehydrates digestion waste; stores feces for defecation (排便). No significant nutrient absorption occurs here.

Exam trap: Chylomicrons enter the lacteal (lymph), not the portal blood — they are too large for capillaries. Short-chain FAs go directly to the portal vein. Many students assume all lipids take the same route.

5. Vitamin Absorption

Vitamins are divided into two categories based on solubility, which determines their absorption mechanism:

Hydrophobic (疏水性) — fat-soluble Vitamins A, D, E, K Absorbed together with lipids — packaged into mixed micelles in the intestinal lumen, diffuse into absorptive cells alongside FFA and MG, and follow the chylomicron route into lymph.
Exam trap: Vitamin B12 is water-soluble but requires intrinsic factor — it cannot be absorbed by carrier proteins alone. Loss of gastric parietal cells (e.g. in autoimmune gastritis) causes B12 deficiency (pernicious anaemia 惡性貧血) even if dietary intake is normal.

6. Mineral Absorption

Sodium (Na⁺) is absorbed along the entire intestine, with the highest absorption rate in the jejunum (空腸) and slower rates in the ileum and colon.

  • Crosses the mucosal membrane (黏膜) down an electrochemical gradient (電化學梯度) via multiple mechanisms: ion channels (離子通道), cotransporters (共同運輸蛋白), and exchangers (交換蛋白)
  • Actively expelled from the epithelial cell at the basolateral membrane by Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase (鈉鉀泵) — maintains the low intracellular Na⁺ concentration that drives continued luminal entry

Calcium (Ca²⁺) absorption switches route depending on dietary intake:

Intake levelRouteMechanism
High dietary intake Paracellular (細胞旁路) — passive diffusion Ca²⁺ diffuses through tight junctions (緊密連接) throughout the small intestine; concentration gradient drives it
Low dietary intake Transcellular (跨細胞) — active, in duodenum & upper jejunum Step 1: Ca²⁺ enters via Ca²⁺ channels in the brush border (刷狀緣) down an electrochemical gradient
Step 2: Cytosolic calbindin (鈣結合蛋白) binds Ca²⁺ — buffers free intracellular Ca²⁺ and shuttles it to the basolateral side

Exam trap: Calcitriol (active vitamin D) regulates all three steps of transcellular Ca²⁺ absorption — channel entry, calbindin shuttling, and basolateral pump extrusion. Vitamin D deficiency therefore impairs Ca²⁺ absorption at all three points simultaneously.

7. Water Absorption & the Large Intestine

Water absorption (水吸收): very little net water absorption occurs in the duodenum (十二指腸); the main site is the jejunum (空腸). Water moves by osmosis (滲透作用), following the transport of "osmotically active" solutes — primarily Na⁺, sugars, and amino acids. Water can pass through:

  • Tight junctions (細胞間隙) — paracellular route
  • Aquaporins (水通道蛋白) — water channels on the plasma membrane

Large intestine (大腸) — anatomy: cecum (盲腸, "blind gut") → colon → rectum. The colon has 4 segments: ascending (升結腸), transverse (橫結腸), descending (降結腸), sigmoid (乙狀結腸).

Functions of the large intestine:

  • Feces formation: processes remaining undigested chyme into relatively solid, bulky feces by absorbing water and electrolytes; enables fecal excretion at appropriate intervals
  • Mucus secretion: protects mucosa from mechanical damage caused by solid content flow; helps mold feces
  • Absorption & secretion: ascending and transverse colons are the active sites — water absorption dehydrates content and consolidates feces
  • Storage: descending and sigmoid colons store feces; once feces reaches the rectum (直腸), the defecation reflex (排便反射) is triggered
  • Fecal color: characteristic brown color results from bilirubin (膽紅素) decomposition by colonic bacteria
Exam trap: The ascending and transverse colons absorb and secrete; the descending and sigmoid colons only store. Students often assume the entire colon is just a storage tube — but the proximal half is still functionally active in absorption.
Exam Traps Summary

  • ORT requires glucose to work — glucose drives SGLT1-mediated Na⁺ and water absorption; salt water alone is far less effective.
  • Dipeptides and tripeptides are the major (faster) protein absorption pathway — not free amino acids.
  • Chylomicrons (long-chain fats) → lacteal/lymph; short-chain FAs (<12C) → portal vein directly.
  • Fructose is absorbed passively; glucose and galactose require SGLT1 (Na⁺ cotransport, active).
  • Carbohydrate absorption is complete by the end of the jejunum — not the ileum.
  • Vitamin B12 is water-soluble but still requires intrinsic factor — carrier proteins alone are insufficient.
  • Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) are absorbed with lipids via the chylomicron/lacteal route.
  • Calcitriol regulates all 3 steps of transcellular Ca²⁺ absorption — channel, calbindin, and basolateral pump.
  • Ascending + transverse colon = absorption/secretion; descending + sigmoid colon = storage only.
  • Fecal brown color comes from bilirubin decomposition — not from undigested food.

  2. Surface area: 0.33 m² → ~200 m²; circular folds ×3, villi ×10, microvilli ×20

Lecture 7: Neuro1: Physiology of Nerve Cells — Ion Channels, Membrane Potential & Action Potentials

Neurons communicate electrically by maintaining steep ion gradients across their membranes and gating selective ion channels to produce rapid voltage changes. This lecture covers how ion channels work, how the resting membrane potential is set, how action potentials are generated and propagated, and how chemical synapses transmit signals from one neuron to the next. Together these mechanisms form the physical basis of all neural signalling in the nervous system.

1. Ion Channels — Structure, Properties & Types

Ion channels (離子通道) are pore-forming membrane proteins (膜孔蛋白) that allow ions to pass through the channel pore (孔道) down their electrochemical gradient (電化學梯度) without metabolic energy input.

  • Two distinctive features separating channels from other transporter proteins:
    1. Rate of ion transport is very high — >106 ions/sec
    2. Ions flow passively (被動運輸) "downhill" along the electrochemical gradient; no ATP or co-transport required
  • Functions of ion channels:
    • Establishing the resting membrane potential (靜息膜電位)
    • Shaping action potentials (動作電位) and other electrical signals
    • Controlling ion flow across secretory (分泌) and epithelial cells (上皮細胞)
    • Regulating cell volume (細胞體積調節)

Membrane transport proteins (膜運輸蛋白) fall into two broad classes: channels and carriers (載體). Channels always mediate passive transport (被動運輸); carriers can be passive or active.

  • Channel: aqueous pore; always passive (facilitated diffusion, 促進擴散)
  • Carrier subtypes:
    • Uniport (單向運輸) — one solute, passive
    • Symport (同向運輸) / cotransporter — two solutes in same direction; secondary active transport (次級主動運輸)
    • Antiport (反向運輸) / exchanger — two solutes in opposite directions; secondary active transport
    • ATP-driven pumps (ATP驅動泵) — primary active transport (初級主動運輸), uses ATP directly

Gating (閘門控制) is the switching of a channel between closed and open conformations (構象); the channel fluctuates stochastically (隨機性地). Four main gating mechanisms exist:

Gating Type Trigger Example
Voltage-gated (電壓閘控) Change in membrane potential Nav, Kv, Cav channels
Ligand-gated — extracellular (胞外配體閘控) Extracellular ligand binding Nicotinic ACh receptor
Ligand-gated — intracellular (胞內配體閘控) Intracellular ligand (e.g. Ca2+, cAMP) Ca2+-dependent K+ channel
Mechanically gated (機械閘控) Membrane stretch or deformation Hair-cell mechanoreceptors

The patch-clamp technique (膜片鉗技術), developed by Erwin Neher and Bernd Sakmann (Nobel Prize 1991), records current through single channels from membrane patches, enabling pharmacology and biophysics studies of individual channels.

Exam trap: Channels are NOT "passive" because they are open; they are passive because ions move strictly down the electrochemical gradient (電化學梯度). Carriers can also be passive — the defining difference is energy requirement, not protein type.
Exam trap: TTX (tetrodotoxin, 河豚毒素) blocks voltage-gated Na+ channels specifically; TEA (tetraethylammonium) blocks K+ channels. Do not mix them up — TTX has no effect on K+ channels.

2. Membrane Potential & Passive Properties

The resting membrane potential (靜息膜電位, Vrest) is the stable voltage difference across the plasma membrane (質膜) at rest, typically around -70 to -80 mV in neurons, negative inside relative to outside.

  • Two requirements to establish a membrane potential:
    1. Semi-permeable membrane (半透膜) — selectively permeable to certain ions
    2. Ion concentration gradient (離子濃度梯度) — the ratio [ion]out/[ion]in must not equal 1

Typical ion concentrations in neurons:

The Nernst equation (能斯特方程) calculates the equilibrium potential (平衡電位) Ex for a single ion — the membrane voltage at which there is no net ion flow:

  • Formula: Ex = (RT/Fz) × ln([X]outside/[X]inside); at body temperature simplifies to 58 mV/z × log([X]out/[X]in)
  • EK for squid: 58 mV/1 × log(20/400) = -75 mV — close to resting potential, explaining why K+ dominates Vrest
  • ENa for squid: 58 mV/1 × log(440/50) = +55 mV — positive, reflecting Na+ chemical gradient pushing inward
  • Equilibrium potential for K+ ≈ resting potential because resting PK is much larger than PNa

The Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz (GHK) equation (高曼-霍奇金-卡茨方程) gives the actual membrane potential when the membrane is permeable to multiple ions, weighting each by its relative permeability (相對滲透性):

  • Vm = (RT/F) × ln[(PK[K+]o + PNa[Na+]o + PCl[Cl-]i) / (PK[K+]i + PNa[Na+]i + PCl[Cl-]o)]
  • At rest, relative permeabilities: PK : PNa : PCl = 1 : 0.05 : 0.45 — K+ dominates
  • Permeability values are reported as relative (unitless); PK is the reference = 1

The Na+/K+-ATPase pump (鈉鉀泵) maintains ion gradients over the long term, pumping 3 Na+ out and 2 K+ in per ATP cycle, sustaining the high [K+]in and high [Na+]out needed for Vrest and AP generation.

  • Without the Na+/K+ pump, repeated APs would gradually dissipate the gradients
  • Large K+ permeability (相對大的K+滲透性) at rest keeps Vm near EK
  • Small Na+ permeability at rest means Vm is slightly more positive than EK
Exam trap: The Nernst equation applies to ONE ion only and gives the equilibrium potential (平衡電位), not the actual resting Vm. To find Vm when multiple ions are permeable, you must use the Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz equation.

3. Action Potential — Generation & Phases

An action potential (動作電位, AP) is a rapid, all-or-none (全有或全無) change in membrane voltage lasting 1–2 ms. It requires a depolarizing trigger (去極化觸發) above threshold (閾值).

  • Phases of the AP:
    1. Resting potential — ~-70 mV
    2. Depolarization (去極化) / rising phase — Na+ channels open; Na+ rushes in; Vm rises to ~+20 mV
    3. Repolarization (復極化) / falling phase — Na+ channels inactivate; K+ channels open; K+ flows out
    4. After-hyperpolarization (後超極化) — K+ channels remain open briefly; Vm dips below resting
    5. Return to resting potential
  • Key characteristics:
    • Short duration: 1–2 ms
    • All-or-none: same shape and amplitude regardless of stimulus strength above threshold
    • Threshold (閾值): must be reached for AP to fire; sub-threshold stimuli produce no AP
    • Peak ~+20 mV; resting ~-70 to -80 mV

Threshold and positive feedback (正回饋) loop at AP initiation:

  1. Depolarization > membrane repolarization
  2. Na+ influx (流入) > K+ efflux (流出) → net depolarization
  3. More depolarization → more voltage-gated Na+ channels open
  4. Positive feedback loop established → explosive AP upstroke
Exam trap: The all-or-none (全有或全無) law means AP amplitude does NOT increase with stronger stimuli — a bigger suprathreshold (超閾值) stimulus does not produce a bigger AP, only a higher firing frequency.

4. Refractory Periods & AP Propagation

After an AP, Na+ channels (鈉通道) pass through inactivated states that create refractory periods (不應期) governing when and how well a second AP can be generated.

  • Absolute refractory period (絕對不應期):
    • Na+ channels fully inactivated (失活)
    • Threshold is effectively infinite — no second AP possible regardless of stimulus strength
    • Sets the upper limit of AP firing frequency
  • Relative refractory period (相對不應期):
    • Na+ channels partially inactivated
    • Threshold elevated — a larger-than-normal stimulus can trigger an AP
    • AP generated has smaller amplitude
  • Normal excitability: Na+ channels fully recovered; threshold back to normal

Sub-threshold signals propagate passively and decrementally (衰減性); APs propagate actively and without decrement (不衰減).

Property Passive (sub-threshold) Propagation Active (AP) Propagation
Amplitude over distance Decays rapidly (length constant, 長度常數 λ) No decrement — regenerated at each patch
Speed Slowed by membrane capacitance (膜電容) Fast; determined by axon diameter and myelination
Direction All directions from source One direction only (unidirectional, 單向)
  • Mechanism of unidirectional propagation: each depolarized patch (膜片) depolarizes the adjacent forward patch; the rear patch is refractory (不應期) and cannot be re-activated
  • Saltatory conduction (跳躍式傳導) in myelinated axons (有髓軸突):
    • Myelin sheath (髓鞘) formed by Schwann cells (許旺細胞) insulates axon segments
    • AP jumps between nodes of Ranvier (蘭氏結), skipping myelinated internodes
    • Faster and more energy-efficient than continuous conduction
    • Na+ channels concentrated at nodes of Ranvier
Exam trap: The absolute refractory period (絕對不應期) is caused by Na+ channel inactivation, NOT by K+ channel opening. K+ channel opening contributes to the after-hyperpolarization and relative refractory period, but is not the primary reason a second AP is impossible during the absolute phase.
Exam trap: Saltatory conduction (跳躍式傳導) is faster than continuous conduction in unmyelinated axons — not slower. Students often confuse the "jumping" metaphor with a delay.

5. Synaptic Transmission

At a chemical synapse (化學突觸), an AP in the presynaptic neuron (突觸前神經元) triggers neurotransmitter (神經遞質) release into the synaptic cleft (突觸間隙), which binds postsynaptic receptors (突觸後受體) to produce electrical changes.

  • Steps of synaptic transmission:
    1. Synthesis (合成) of neurotransmitter — in cell body, transported to terminal
    2. Storage (儲存) in synaptic vesicles (突觸小泡); vesicles transported to active zone (活躍區)
    3. Release / exocytosis (胞吐) — depolarization → Ca2+ influx → vesicle fusion → transmitter release into cleft
    4. Postsynaptic effects (突觸後效應) — transmitter binds receptor; ion channels open; electrical response
    5. Inactivation (失活) — degradation in cleft (e.g. acetylcholinesterase, 乙醯膽鹼酯酶) or presynaptic re-uptake (再攝取) of monoamines

Molecular mechanism of vesicle release involves SNARE proteins (可溶性NSF附著受體蛋白) and synaptotagmin (突觸結合蛋白):

  • Vesicle moves to active zone (actin-mediated)
  • v-SNARE on vesicle and t-SNARE on membrane form a complex that docks vesicle
  • Ca2+ rise in cytoplasm (detected by synaptotagmin) triggers final fusion and exocytosis

The nature of the postsynaptic receptor (突觸後受體) determines whether the response is excitatory or inhibitory:

Response Abbreviation Voltage change Mechanism Effect on AP
Excitatory postsynaptic potential (興奮性突觸後電位) EPSP Depolarization (去極化) Opening of cation channels (Na+, K+) Brings membrane closer to threshold
Inhibitory postsynaptic potential (抑制性突觸後電位) IPSP Hyperpolarization (超極化) Opening of Cl- or K+ channels Moves membrane away from threshold; prevents AP

Integration (整合) of synaptic input occurs at the axon hillock (軸突丘), the spike initiation zone (動作電位起始區):

  • Excitatory and inhibitory inputs summate spatially (空間加和) and temporally (時間加和) at the dendrites and soma
  • Net depolarizing current flows to axon hillock; if threshold is reached, AP fires
  • AP then propagates by saltatory conduction down the axon

Major classes of neurotransmitters (神經遞質類型):

  • Acetylcholine (乙醯膽鹼, ACh) — classic neuromuscular transmitter
  • Amino acids (氨基酸): GABA (γ-胺基丁酸, inhibitory), Glycine (甘胺酸, inhibitory), Glutamic acid (谷氨酸, excitatory)
  • Monoamines (單胺類): Dopamine (多巴胺), Norepinephrine (去甲腎上腺素), Epinephrine (腎上腺素), Octopamine (章魚胺), Serotonin (血清素)
  • Peptides (肽類): Met-Enkephalin (甲硫氨酸腦啡肽), Substance P (P物質), Angiotensin II, Oxytocin (催產素), Somatostatin (生長抑素), Vasopressin (升壓素), Opioids, Glucagon, Neurotensin, Proctolin
  • Gases: Nitric oxide / NO (一氧化氮), Carbon monoxide / CO (一氧化碳)
  • Nucleotides (核苷酸): ATP (三磷酸腺苷), GTP (三磷酸鳥苷)

Termination of postsynaptic transmitter action — four mechanisms:

  1. Diffusion (擴散) away from synaptic cleft — mainly peptide transmitters
  2. Enzymatic degradation (酶解) in cleft — e.g. acetylcholinesterase breaks down ACh
  3. Re-uptake (再攝取) into presynaptic terminal — e.g. monoamines (recycling)
  4. Combination of mechanisms 1–3
Exam trap: EPSP and IPSP are determined by the postsynaptic receptor type, not by the transmitter identity alone. The same transmitter (e.g. ACh) can be excitatory at one synapse and inhibitory at another, depending on which receptor subtype is expressed.
Exam trap: Ca2+ is required for vesicle fusion and exocytosis (胞吐) — without Ca2+ influx into the presynaptic terminal, no transmitter is released even if an AP arrives.
Exam Traps Summary
  • Channels are passive because ions move down the electrochemical gradient — not because the channel protein is inert; carriers can also be passive.
  • TTX blocks voltage-gated Na+ channels; TEA blocks K+ channels — never swap these two blockers.
  • The Nernst equation is for ONE ion only — use Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz when multiple ions contribute to Vm.
  • All-or-none means AP amplitude is constant above threshold — stronger stimuli increase firing rate, not AP size.
  • The absolute refractory period is due to Na+ channel inactivation, not K+ channel opening.
  • Saltatory conduction is faster than continuous conduction, not slower.
  • EPSP vs IPSP is determined by the postsynaptic receptor type, not the transmitter identity.
  • Ca2+ influx into the presynaptic terminal is mandatory for vesicle exocytosis — no Ca2+, no transmitter release.
Top things to memorize
  1. Two features of ion channels vs carriers: rate >106 ions/sec; passive (no ATP), along electrochemical gradient.
  2. Nernst equation: Ex = 58 mV/z × log([X]out/[X]in); EK = -75 mV (squid), ENa = +55 mV (squid).
  3. Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz permeability ratio at rest: PK : PNa : PCl = 1 : 0.05 : 0.45.
  4. TTX blocks voltage-gated Na+ channels (rising phase of AP); TEA blocks K+ channels (repolarization).
  5. AP phases: resting → depolarization (Na+ in) → repolarization (K+ out) → after-hyperpolarization → resting; duration 1–2 ms.
  6. Absolute refractory period: Na+ channels inactivated, no AP possible; relative refractory: Na+ partially inactivated, larger stimulus needed.
  7. Saltatory conduction: AP jumps between nodes of Ranvier (蘭氏結); faster and more energy-efficient.
  8. Synaptic transmission sequence: synthesis → storage → Ca2+-triggered exocytosis → receptor binding → electrical response → inactivation.
  9. EPSP = depolarization (cation channel opening); IPSP = hyperpolarization (Cl- or K+ channel opening); determined by receptor type.
  10. Na+/K+-ATPase: pumps 3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in per ATP; maintains long-term ion gradients essential for Vrest.
  11. Neurotransmitter classes: ACh, amino acids (GABA, glycine, glutamate), monoamines (dopamine, NE, serotonin), peptides, gases (NO, CO), nucleotides (ATP, GTP).
  12. Integration at axon hillock: spatial + temporal summation of EPSPs and IPSPs determines if AP threshold is reached.

Lecture 8: Neuro2: Properties of Sensory Cells and Sensory Systems

Sensory physiology explains how specialized receptor cells convert (轉換) environmental stimuli into electrical signals the brain can interpret. Each receptor type is tuned to a specific energy form, and the signal passes through a defined neural pathway before reaching conscious awareness. This lecture covers the core principles of sensory transduction, coding, and the specific properties of somatic touch, temperature, pain, and hearing receptors. Understanding these principles is foundational for linking receptor biology to clinical conditions such as hearing loss, chronic pain, and proprioceptive deficits.

1. Sensory Receptor Types and Transmission Patterns

A sensory receptor (感覺受器) is a specialized cell — neural or non-neural — that detects a specific environmental stimulus (刺激) and converts it into an electrical signal processable by the CNS. Receptor selectivity is achieved through selective receptor structures (選擇性受器結構) and intracellular amplification (細胞內放大).

Receptor Type (受器類型) Stimuli Examples
Mechanoreceptors (機械受器) Pressure (baroreceptors), stretch (osmoreceptors), vibration, acceleration, sound
Photoreceptors (光受器) Photons of light
Thermoreceptors (溫度受器) Varying degrees of heat
Chemoreceptors (化學受器) pH, O₂, osmolarity, taste, smell molecules
Nociceptors (傷害受器) Chemical, mechanical, or thermal stimuli causing tissue damage

Three basic patterns (基本傳遞模式) exist for how receptor cells relay signals to the CNS:

  • Type 1 — Neural receptor; stimulus directly generates a generator potential (產生電位) that triggers action potentials (動作電位) at the spike-initiating zone (尖峰起始區) of the same neuron (e.g., olfactory receptor cells, vertebrate type)
  • Type 2 — Neural receptor with a chemical synapse (化學突觸) between the receptor membrane and the afferent neuron; receptor potential triggers neurotransmitter (神經傳遞物) release onto a secondary afferent neuron
  • Type 3 — Non-neural receptor cell (非神經受器細胞); receptor potential drives neurotransmitter release onto a downstream afferent neuron that then fires action potentials (e.g., taste bud cells, hair cells)
Exam trap: Type 1 and Type 2 both involve neural receptors, but only Type 2 has a chemical synapse between receptor membrane and the afferent axon. Type 3 uses a non-neural receptor cell — the receptor itself never fires an action potential; only the downstream neuron does.

2. Sensory Transduction and Sensory Coding

Sensory transduction (感覺轉導) is the conversion of stimulus energy (刺激能量) into information processable by the CNS. Environmental stimuli depolarize (去極化) the membrane by opening ion channels (離子通道).

  • Neural receptors: depolarization produces a generator potential (產生電位) → triggers action potential at spike-initiating zone
  • Non-neural receptors: depolarization produces a receptor potential (受器電位) → neurotransmitter release → AP in downstream afferent neuron

Detailed transduction cascade (轉導串聯) steps:

  1. Stimulus reaches receptor cells → receptor protein (受器蛋白) is activated
  2. Cascade of protein interactions modifies intracellular second messengers (第二傳訊者)
  3. Ion channels open (or close) → receptor current (受器電流) changes membrane potential (膜電位, Vm)
  4. Change in Vm spreads electrotonically (電緊張性) to spike-initiating zone (Types 1 & 2) or drives transmitter release (Type 3)
  5. Number and/or frequency of APs conducted along the axon changes → information reaches CNS

Sensory coding (感覺編碼) for stimulus intensity and duration:

  • Strength and duration of the generator potential (or receptor potential) vary with stimulus intensity (刺激強度) and duration (刺激持續時間)
  • Frequency of APs (or amount of neurotransmitter secretion) is proportional to stimulus intensity
  • Duration of a series of APs (or neurotransmitter secretion) is proportional to stimulus duration

General properties of sensory systems (感覺系統一般特性) include absolute refractory period (絕對不反應期) and relative refractory period (相對不反應期) of action potentials, which set limits on maximum signaling frequency.

3. Somatic Senses: Touch, Proprioception, Temperature, and Nociception

Four somatosensory modalities (體感模式): Touch (觸覺), Proprioception (本體感覺), Temperature (溫度覺), and Nociception (傷害感覺) — pain and itching.

Somatosensory pathway (體感傳導路徑):

  • Receptor activation → action potentials in primary sensory neurons (第一級感覺神經元)
  • Primary neurons → secondary sensory neurons (第二級感覺神經元, interneurons); all secondary neurons cross the midline (中線) so left-body sensations are processed in right hemisphere (右大腦半球) and vice versa
  • In the thalamus (視丘), secondary neurons synapse onto tertiary sensory neurons (第三級感覺神經元) → project to somatosensory cortex (體感皮質)

Somatosensory cortex (體感皮質) properties:

  • Recognizes the location (位置) of sensation; each body part maps to a corresponding cortical region
  • Cortical area devoted to a body part is proportional (成比例) to that part's sensitivity (感敏度) — not its physical size
  • Cortical map size is not fixed; it changes according to usage (使用頻率) of the body part

Touch receptors (觸覺受器) respond to stretch, steady pressure, fluttering, vibration, and texture; come as free nerve endings (自由神經末梢) and complex encapsulated receptors:

Receptor Location Function
Merkel receptors (默克爾受器) Superficial skin Steady pressure and texture
Meissner's corpuscle (邁斯納小體) Superficial skin Flutter and stroking movements
Pacinian corpuscle (帕欽尼小體) Deep skin Vibration
Ruffini corpuscle (魯菲尼小體) Deep skin Skin stretch
Free nerve ending of hair root (毛根自由神經末梢) Around hair follicles Hair movement
Free nerve ending of nociceptor (傷害受器自由末梢) Skin layers Noxious stimuli
  • Piezo2 (壓電蛋白2) — mechanosensitive channel (機械敏感通道) that mediates gentle touch; present in Merkel cells and LTMR (low-threshold mechanoreceptor) afferents
  • Piezo2 is also the principal mechanotransduction channel (機械轉導通道) for proprioception (本體感覺) — patients with PIEZO2 mutation have impaired touch and proprioception especially when eyes are closed
  • Discovery of Piezo channels (along with TRPV1/TRPM8) earned the 2021 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine

Temperature receptors (溫度受器) are free nerve endings; TRP (瞬時感受器電位) channels mediate transduction:

  • TRPM8 (瞬時感受器電位M8) — cold receptor (冷受器); responds to temperatures below body temperature; also activated by menthol (薄荷醇) and eucalyptol (桉樹腦); TRPM8-deficient mice are insensitive to cold
  • TRPV1 (瞬時感受器電位V1) — noxious heat (有害熱) over 43°C; also activated by capsaicin (辣椒素) from chili peppers
  • Warm receptors: body temperature to ~45°C; mediated by various TRP channels (TRPM3, TRPA1, TRPV1, and others)
  • Discovery of TRPV1 (David Julius) and TRPM8 (Ardem Patapoutian) as temperature sensors led to the 2021 Nobel Prize
Exam trap: TRPV1 responds to both noxious heat (>43°C) AND capsaicin — hot chili feels "hot" because capsaicin hijacks the same channel as thermal heat. TRPM8 responds to cold AND to cooling compounds (menthol, eucalyptol). These channels are not exclusive to their named stimulus.

Nociceptors (傷害受器 / 痛覺受器):

  • Also called pain receptors (痛覺受器); free nerve endings that respond to chemical, mechanical, or thermal stimuli strong enough to cause or risk tissue damage (組織損傷)
  • Activate two pathways: (A) reflexive protective responses (保護性反射) integrated at the spinal cord level, and (B) ascending pathways (上行路徑) to the cortex that become conscious pain sensation
Exam trap: All secondary sensory neurons cross the midline — sensations from the left body are processed in the right hemisphere. The crossing happens at different levels for different modalities but the rule is universal for somatosensory signals.

4. Hearing: A Special Sense

Hearing (聽覺) is our perception of energy carried by sound waves (聲波), including frequency (頻率), amplitude (振幅), and complexity (複雜度).

  • Disabling hearing loss affects 5.3% of world population and one-third of persons over 65
  • The molecular identity of the mechanotransduction channel (機械轉導通道) in auditory hair cells is not yet fully established — considered the "last frontier" of sensory neurobiology

Three physical dimensions of sound (聲音物理維度) and their perceptual correlates (感知對應):

Physical Dimension (物理維度) Physical Stimulus Perceptual Dimension (感知維度)
Amplitude (振幅) Loud ↔ Soft waveform height Loudness (響度)
Frequency (頻率) Low ↔ High cycles/sec Pitch (音調)
Complexity (複雜度) Pure ↔ Rich waveform Timbre (音色, 音質)
  • Amplitude: normal conversation ≈ 60 dB; sounds >80 dB can damage hair cells (毛細胞)
  • Frequency: average human ear hears 20–20,000 Hz; most acute hearing at 1–3 kHz
  • Timbre (音色) — property that makes the same pitch and loudness sound different from different instruments or voices
  • Frequency range and sensitivity decrease with age, especially at high frequencies; men tend to lose high-frequency hearing earlier than women

Gross anatomy of the ear (耳部解剖):

  • Outer ear (外耳): concha, external auditory canal — funnels sound to tympanic membrane (鼓膜)
  • Middle ear (中耳): three ossicles (聽小骨) — malleus (錘骨), incus (砧骨), stapes (鐙骨) — transfer vibrations from tympanic membrane to oval window (卵圓窗); Eustachian tube (歐氏管) equalizes pressure
  • Inner ear (內耳): cochlea (耳蝸) contains the organ of Corti (柯蒂氏器); vestibular organ (前庭器官) for balance; vestibulocochlear nerve (前庭耳蝸神經)

Cochlear structure (耳蝸結構):

  • Scala vestibuli (前庭階) — perilymph (外淋巴); runs from oval window to helicotrema (蝸頂孔) at apex
  • Cochlear duct / scala media (耳蝸管/中階) — endolymph (內淋巴); contains organ of Corti
  • Scala tympani (鼓階) — perilymph; runs from helicotrema back to round window (圓窗)
  • Reissner's membrane (萊斯納氏膜) separates scala vestibuli from cochlear duct; basilar membrane (基底膜) separates cochlear duct from scala tympani
  • Organ of Corti sits on basilar membrane; covered by tectorial membrane (蓋膜)
  • Inner hair cells (IHC, 內毛細胞): 1 row, ~3,000 per cochlea — primary sensory transducers
  • Outer hair cells (OHC, 外毛細胞): 3 rows, ~12,000 per cochlea — amplifiers

Sound transmission pathway (聲音傳導路徑):

  1. Sound waves strike tympanic membrane → become vibrations
  2. Vibrations transferred to three ossicles of middle ear
  3. Stapes (鐙骨) attached to oval window membrane → vibrations create fluid waves (液體波) in cochlea
  4. Fluid waves push on flexible membranes of cochlear duct
  5. Energy transfers across cochlear duct into tympanic duct → dissipated back into middle ear at round window
  6. Hair cells within cochlear duct create action potentials in cochlear nerve (耳蝸神經) sensory neurons
Exam trap: IHCs (3,000) are the primary sensory transducers with nearly 10:1 fiber-to-IHC innervation by cochlear nerve fibers; OHCs (12,000) are amplifiers via electromotility, not the primary signal senders. Most cochlear nerve fibers (30,000+) innervate IHCs, not OHCs. Do not confuse the larger number of OHCs with greater innervation.

5. Auditory Transduction, Hair Cell Mechanisms, and Frequency Coding

Mechano-electrical transduction (機械電轉導) in hair cells:

  • Hair cells have stereocilia (纖毛束) with tip links (頂端連結蛋白) that gate ion channels — described as "trap doors"
  • At rest: ~10% of channels are open → tonic depolarizing current → tonic neurotransmitter release → baseline AP firing in sensory neuron
  • Excitation (興奮): stereocilia deflect toward tallest cilia → tip links pull channels open → ion influx → depolarization → increased NT release → increased AP frequency
  • Inhibition (抑制): deflection in opposite direction → channels close → hyperpolarization (超極化) → decreased NT release → decreased AP frequency
  • The mechanotransduction channel complex (機械轉導通道複合體) includes TMC1 (transmembrane channel-like protein 1) and CIB2 (calcium- and integrin-binding protein 2) — identified by Qiu X and Muller, Neuron 2022

Outer hair cell amplification (外毛細胞放大) — electromotility (電動性):

  • Cochlear nerve: 30,000+ fibers; nearly 10:1 fiber-to-IHC innervation ratio; sparse fibers carry OHC info to brain
  • Small number of efferent fibers (傳出神經纖維) descend from brain to OHCs to modulate their activity
  • Prestin (前庭蛋白) — transmembrane motor protein in OHCs; mechanically contracts and elongates in response to Vm changes (電壓變化) → drives electromotility (電動性) of OHCs
  • Electromotility is the driving force of cochlear amplification (耳蝸放大), which increases sensitivity to incoming sound wave frequencies and amplifies the signal

Sensory coding for sound frequency — Place Theory (位置理論):

  • Each place (位置) along the basilar membrane (基底膜) corresponds to perception of a given frequency (特定頻率)
  • High frequencies activate hair cells near the base (基部) of the cochlea; low frequencies activate cells near the apex (頂部) / helicotrema
  • Developed by Georg von Békésy (1899–1972)
  • The traveling wave (行進波) along the basilar membrane peaks at a frequency-specific location — this is the physical basis of place coding
Exam trap: Place theory maps frequency to basilar membrane location — high frequency near the BASE, low frequency near the APEX. Students commonly reverse this. The base is narrower and stiffer, resonating at high frequencies; the apex is wider and more flexible, resonating at low frequencies.

6. Conscious vs. Subconscious Sensory Processing and Overview

Sensory information is processed either consciously (有意識) or subconsciously (潛意識):

Conscious Processing (有意識處理) Subconscious Processing (潛意識處理)
Special senses: Vision, Hearing, Taste, Smell, Equilibrium Muscle length and tension, Proprioception
Somatic senses: Touch, Temperature, Pain, Itch, Proprioception Visceral stimuli: Blood pressure, blood pH, O₂ content
pH of cerebrospinal fluid, lung inflation, osmolarity, blood glucose, GI distension, temperature
  • Proprioception (本體感覺) — sense of self-movement (自我運動) and body position (身體位置); can be both conscious and subconscious
  • PIEZO2 mutation patients demonstrate the importance of proprioception: with eyes open, movement is normal; with eyes closed, movement becomes disorganized — showing the brain uses visual feedback to compensate for lost proprioceptive input

Summary of key channel–stimulus relationships (通道與刺激對應):

  • Piezo2 — gentle touch + proprioception; 2021 Nobel Prize (Patapoutian)
  • TRPV1 — noxious heat (>43°C) + capsaicin; 2021 Nobel Prize (Julius)
  • TRPM8 — cold + menthol/eucalyptol; 2021 Nobel Prize (Patapoutian)
  • TMC1/CIB2 complex — auditory hair cell mechanotransduction
  • Prestin — OHC electromotility; cochlear amplification
Exam Traps Summary
  • Type 1 vs. Type 2 transmission: both use neural receptors, but only Type 2 has a chemical synapse between the receptor membrane and afferent neuron; Type 3 uses a non-neural receptor cell that never fires its own AP.
  • TRPV1 responds to both heat (>43°C) AND capsaicin; TRPM8 responds to both cold AND menthol/eucalyptol — these channels are not exclusive to one stimulus type.
  • All secondary somatosensory neurons cross the midline — left-body sensations are processed in the right hemisphere.
  • Cortical map area is proportional to sensitivity, not body size — lips and fingertips have disproportionately large cortical representations.
  • IHCs (3,000) are the primary signal senders with ~10:1 innervation; OHCs (12,000) are mechanical amplifiers via prestin/electromotility. The larger cell count of OHCs does NOT mean more innervation.
  • Place theory: high frequency maps to the BASE of the cochlea; low frequency maps to the APEX — students commonly reverse this.
  • At rest, ~10% of hair cell channels are open, maintaining tonic activity — hair cells are not silent at baseline.
  • The molecular identity of the auditory hair cell mechanotransduction channel (TMC1/CIB2 complex) is still under active research and not yet fully established.
Top things to memorize
  1. Three transmission types: Type 1 (neural, no synapse), Type 2 (neural, chemical synapse), Type 3 (non-neural receptor cell)
  2. Sensory coding: AP frequency ∝ stimulus intensity; AP duration ∝ stimulus duration
  3. Somatosensory pathway: primary neuron → secondary neuron (crosses midline) → thalamus → tertiary neuron → somatosensory cortex
  4. Six touch receptor types and their functions: Merkel (steady pressure/texture), Meissner's (flutter/stroking), Pacinian (vibration), Ruffini (stretch), hair root free ending (hair movement), nociceptor free ending (noxious stimuli)
  5. TRPV1 = heat >43°C + capsaicin; TRPM8 = cold + menthol/eucalyptol; both earned 2021 Nobel Prize
  6. Piezo2 = gentle touch + proprioception; also 2021 Nobel Prize
  7. IHC: 3,000 cells, 1 row, ~10:1 fiber innervation — primary signal transducers; OHC: 12,000 cells, 3 rows, sparse innervation — mechanical amplifiers via prestin/electromotility
  8. Sound transmission: tympanic membrane → malleus → incus → stapes → oval window → cochlear fluid waves → basilar membrane → hair cells → cochlear nerve
  9. Place theory (von Békésy): high frequency = cochlear base; low frequency = cochlear apex
  10. Hair cell at rest: ~10% channels open → tonic signal; deflection toward tallest stereocilia = excitation (depolarization); opposite = inhibition (hyperpolarization)
  11. Prestin — motor protein in OHCs; contracts/elongates with Vm changes → electromotility → cochlear amplification
  12. Human hearing: 20–20,000 Hz; most acute 1–3 kHz; >80 dB risks hair cell damage
