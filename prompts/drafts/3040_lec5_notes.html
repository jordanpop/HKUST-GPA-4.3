Lecture 5: GI2 — Digestion: Mouth, Esophagus, Stomach, Pancreas & Liver

GI Lecture 2 traces the path of food through each digestive organ from mouth to duodenum, covering the mechanical and chemical processes at each site. Every structure — from salivary amylase to the parietal cell's proton pump — is precisely specialized, and understanding their secretions and regulation explains why clinical conditions such as GERD, H. pylori ulcers, and gallstones arise exactly where they do.

1. Mouth & Salivary Glands

The mouth (口腔 / buccal cavity 頰腔) contains three main structures:

  • Teeth (牙齒) — biting, tearing, grinding (mechanical digestion)
  • Tongue (舌頭) — tasting and manipulating food bolus (食團); involved in swallowing
  • Salivary glands (唾液腺) — three pairs: parotid (腮腺), sublingual (舌下腺), submandibular (頜下腺); secrete multifunctional saliva (~1.5 L/day)

Two cell types in salivary glands:

Cell typeSecretionContents
Serous cells (漿液腺細胞)Watery secretionElectrolytes (電解質), salivary amylase (唾液澱粉酶), antibacterial proteins
Mucous cells (黏液腺細胞)Mucins (黏蛋白) / glycoproteins (糖蛋白) → mix with serous → viscous mucus (saliva)Mucins

Three functions of saliva (唾液):

  • A. Moistening & lubrication (潤滑) — essential for swallowing and taste
  • B. Protect teeth against dental caries (蛀牙): contains enzyme proteins (peroxidase 過氧化物酶, lysozyme 溶菌酶) and antibacterial proteins (defensins 防禦素); saliva is slightly alkaline (弱鹼性) — neutralizes acids from mouth bacteria and acid refluxed from stomach
  • C. Amylase — begins starch digestion: starch → maltose (麥芽糖, 2 glucose), maltotriose (麥芽三糖, 3 glucose), oligosaccharides (寡醣, >3 glucose)

Defensins (防禦素): secreted by immune cells and epithelial cells; amphipathic (兩親性) and cationic (陽離子); mechanism: disrupt pathogen membrane (破壞病原體細胞膜), block viral receptors and signaling.

Regulation of saliva: salivary centers in brain stem receive two types of stimuli: (A) touch (pressoreceptors 壓力感受器), smell, and taste (taste buds 味蕾); (B) psychological stimuli (e.g. thought of food = conditioned reflex 條件反射); fear inhibits; sleep, fatigue (疲勞), and dehydration (脫水) also inhibit (→ dry mouth). Salivary glands controlled by autonomic nerves (自主神經):

  • Parasympathetic (facial & glossopharyngeal nerves) — promotes moderate amount of watery saliva; keeps mouth, lips, tongue moist
  • Sympathetic — also stimulates secretion but less than parasympathetic; saliva is richer in mucins (mucin-rich 富黏蛋白), more viscous; sympathetic stimulus → dry mouth sensation

Magnitudes of GI secretion (L/day): Saliva 1.5 | Gastric juice 1–3 | Pancreatic juice 2 | Bile 0.6–1.2 | Intestinal secretions 2 | Total 7–10 L/day. Water lost in feces: ~100 ml/day (the rest is reabsorbed).

Exam trap: Sympathetic stimulation of salivary glands still produces saliva, but it is more viscous and mucin-rich — not absent. The subjective sensation is dry mouth because of less total fluid.

2. Pharynx, Esophagus & Swallowing

The pharynx (咽) has three parts: nasopharynx (鼻咽, air only), oropharynx (口咽), laryngopharynx (喉咽). It is a common passageway for food, fluids, and air; the external muscle layer contains two skeletal muscle layers (pharyngeal constrictor muscles 咽縮肌) involved in the swallowing reflex.

Swallowing reflex (吞嚥反射 / deglutition 吞嚥) — sequential steps:

  1. Tongue moves bolus to rear of mouth → presence of object activates swallowing reflex in pharynx
  2. Soft palate (軟顎) closes nasal passage; epiglottis (會厭) moves over glottis (聲門) to close larynx (喉) and trachea (氣管)
  3. Upper esophageal sphincter (上食道括約肌) relaxes → food bolus propelled into esophagus → sphincter re-contracts

The esophagus (食道) is a collapsible muscular tube (~10 inches / ~25 cm) posterior to the trachea; conducts food to stomach by peristalsis (蠕動). Key features:

  • Begins at inferior end of laryngopharynx; passes through diaphragm (橫膈膜) via esophageal hiatus (食道裂孔)
  • Mucosa = stratified squamous epithelium (多層扁平上皮) / smooth muscle (平滑肌)
  • Upper esophageal sphincter (上食道括約肌) — controls passage from laryngopharynx into esophagus
  • Lower esophageal (cardiac) sphincter (下食道括約肌) — controls passage from esophagus into stomach

GERD (胃食道逆流病): failure of lower esophageal sphincter to close after food enters stomach → acid backflow → "heartburn" (胃灼熱) sensation. Affects ~10–20% of Western populations. Risk factors: obesity (肥胖), pregnancy (懷孕), smoking (吸煙).

Exam trap: The epiglottis covers the glottis (trachea opening) — not the esophagus — during swallowing. The esophageal sphincter relaxes to allow food entry. These two events happen simultaneously but are mechanically distinct.

3. Stomach — Structure & Gastric Juice

The stomach (胃) is a J-shaped enlargement of the GI tract connecting the esophagus to the duodenum (十二指腸). Four functions: (1) food reservoir (食物儲存庫), (2) mechanical fragmentation (機械性分解), (3) mix food with gastric secretion → chyme (食糜), (4) empty gastric contents into small intestine at a controlled rate.

Gross structure (大體結構):

  • Cardia (賁門, "near the heart") — entry from esophagus
  • Fundus (胃底)
  • Body (胃體) — midportion
  • Pyloric region (幽門區, funnel-shaped): pyloric antrum (幽門竇 "cave"), pyloric canal (幽門管), pylorus (幽門) with pyloric sphincter (幽門括約肌) that controls stomach emptying

Rugae (胃皺褶): large folds in the mucosa of the empty stomach; enable expansion (膨脹) and distension (伸展).

Five cell types in gastric glands (胃腺) — collectively produce gastric juice (胃液) at 1–3 L/day:

Cell typeSecretionFunction
Mucous surface cells (黏液表面細胞)Mucus + bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻)Physical & chemical protection of stomach wall
Mucous neck cells (黏液頸細胞)MucusLubrication
Parietal cells (壁細胞)HCl + intrinsic factor (內因子)Protein denaturation, microbial kill; B12 absorption
Chief / zymogenic cells (主細胞)Pepsinogen (胃蛋白酶原) + gastric lipase (胃脂肪酶)Protein & fat digestion
Enteroendocrine G cells (G 細胞)Gastrin (胃泌素)Stimulate HCl secretion, gastric gland growth, motility

Gastric juice — five components:

  • 1. Mucus — viscous (黏稠), slippery; chiefly mucin, water, inorganic salts. Protective barrier against mechanical and chemical damage. Contains HCO₃⁻ (from surface mucous cells) maintaining a HCO₃⁻ gradient — lumen pH ~2 but pH at cell surface ~7.
  • 2. Pepsinogen → pepsin (胃蛋白酶) — converted at pH < 5; cleaves proteins (蛋白質) → small peptides; maximal activity at pH ~2; pepsin can autocatalyze its own formation from pepsinogen.
  • 3. Gastric lipase — initiates fat digestion: TG → diglyceride + fatty acid; accounts for 10–30% of triglyceride digestion.
  • 4. Intrinsic factor (內因子) — glycoprotein (糖蛋白) that binds vitamin B12; essential for B12 absorption in the ileum (迴腸); needed for RBC production (紅血球生成).
  • 5. HCl — denatures proteins, breaks down food by strong acid; kills ingested microbes; converts pepsinogen to pepsin.

Parietal cell HCl secretion mechanism (壁細胞分泌機制) — three transport processes:

  • 1. H⁺/K⁺ ATPase (proton pump 質子幫浦) — apical; pumps H⁺ into lumen, K⁺ into cell (active, ATP-driven)
  • 2. Cl⁻/HCO₃⁻ exchanger (anion exchanger 陰離子交換蛋白) — basolateral; HCO₃⁻ exits to blood, Cl⁻ enters cell
  • 3. Cl⁻ channel — apical; Cl⁻ exits into lumen alongside H⁺ → HCl formed in lumen
  • Source of H⁺: CO₂ + H₂O → H₂CO₃ → HCO₃⁻ + H⁺, catalyzed by carbonic anhydrase (碳酸酐酶, CA)
Exam trap: Pepsinogen requires pH < 5 for conversion to pepsin, but pepsin's optimal activity is at pH ~2. Do not confuse conversion pH with optimal activity pH.

4. Gastric Acid Regulation & H. pylori

Three major stimuli for gastric acid secretion (all synergistic 協同; all act via cAMP or Ca²⁺ signaling → stimulate H⁺/K⁺ ATPase):

StimulusTypeSourceNotes
Histamine (組胺)Paracrine (旁分泌)ECL cells (enterochromaffin-like cells 類腸嗜鉻細胞) in gastric gland epitheliumECL cells are also target of gastrin
Acetylcholine (乙醯膽鹼, ACh)Neurotransmitter (神經傳導物質)Vagal (迷走神經) parasympathetic postganglionic terminalsCephalic and gastric phase signals
Gastrin (胃泌素)Endocrine (內分泌)G cells in pyloric antrum (also duodenum and pancreas)Released by stomach distension + protein digestion products; also stimulates gastric gland growth and gastric motility

Phases of gastric secretion (胃分泌階段):

  • Cephalic phase (頭期) — nervous system control; triggered by sight, smell, taste, or thought of food; chewing and swallowing
  • Gastric phase (胃期) — food enters stomach; stretch (distension 膨脹) and chemoreceptors (amino acid/peptide)
  • Intestinal phase (腸期) — food enters intestine; amino acids stimulate acid secretion via circulation; distension stimulatory

Cimetidine (西咪替丁): H₂ histamine receptor blocker (組胺H₂受體阻斷劑) on parietal cells; one of most popular medicines worldwide; prevents heartburn in overproduction of gastric acid.

Helicobacter pylori (幽門螺旋桿菌, H. pylori): bacterium that survives the harsh stomach environment; uses urease (尿素酶) to convert urea → NH₃ + CO₂, neutralizing gastric acid around itself; penetrates mucus layer; ~50% of world population affected; significantly increases risk of gastric ulcer (胃潰瘍).

Exam trap: The three stimuli (histamine, ACh, gastrin) are synergistic — their combined effect is greater than the sum of individual effects. Blocking just one (e.g. histamine via cimetidine) can substantially reduce total acid output.

5. Pancreas & Liver/Bile

The pancreas (胰腺) is a soft oblong gland ~6 in. long / 1 in. thick; posterior to the greater curvature of the stomach; connected to the duodenum by pancreatic (duct of Wirsung 胰管) and accessory ducts (duct of Santorini) via the sphincter of Oddi (奧迪括約肌).

Dual function: endocrine (內分泌, ~1% of gland = islets of Langerhans 蘭格漢斯島: α/β/δ cells for glucagon/insulin/somatostatin) and exocrine (外分泌, 99% = acinar cells + duct cells).

Exocrine pancreas — two secretory cell types:

  • Duct epithelial cells (導管上皮細胞) — secrete large volume watery solution rich in NaHCO₃; neutralizes gastric acid entering duodenum; provides alkaline pH optimal for enzyme activity; flushes sticky protein secretions from acinar cells
  • Acinar cells (腺泡細胞) — secrete small volume of digestive enzymes
  • Combined = pancreatic juice (胰液) at 1–2 L/day

Digestive enzymes in pancreatic juice:

Most enzymes secreted as inactive proenzymes / zymogens (酶原) activated by trypsin proteolysis. Enteropeptidase (腸激酶, a transmembrane protein at brush border of duodenum/jejunum) converts trypsinogen → trypsin, which then activates all other pancreatic zymogens (cascade 級聯反應).

The liver (肝臟) is the largest gland in the body; 2 main lobes (葉) made of lobules (肝小葉). Multifunctional: controls overall body metabolism (非消化功能); bile formation (膽汁形成) is the major exocrine/digestive function.

Secretion of bile (膽汁分泌): hepatocytes (肝細胞) secrete ~1 L bile/day → bile canaliculi (膽小管) → bile ducts → hepatic ducts. Bile flow pathway:

  • Between meals: bile diverted via cystic duct (膽囊管) → gallbladder (膽囊) for storage and concentration (by removing water)
  • During a meal: gallbladder contracts → bile through common bile duct (總膽管) + relaxed sphincter of Oddi → duodenum

Composition of bile (alkaline solution 鹼性溶液): H₂O (97%), NaCl/NaHCO₃, bile salts (膽鹽), cholesterol (膽固醇), phospholipids (磷脂), bile pigments (膽色素).

  • Bile salts: amphipathic (兩親性), made from cholesterol in liver. (A) Emulsify fats (乳化脂肪) in SI — break large lipid globules → smaller droplets accessible to pancreatic lipase. (B) Solubilize cholesterol/phospholipids in bile → prevent gallstones (膽結石).
  • Bile pigments: bilirubin (膽紅素) = breakdown product of hemoglobin (血紅素) → gives bile yellow-green color. High blood bilirubin → jaundice (黃疸). Bile is both an excretory product (bile pigment) and a digestive secretion (bile salts).

Gallstones (膽結石, cholesterol stones): possible causes — enzyme defect, decreased secretion of bile salts, or increased cholesterol synthesis → cholesterol precipitates.

Exam trap: Enteropeptidase (腸激酶) is an intestinal brush border enzyme — not a pancreatic enzyme. It initiates the activation cascade by converting trypsinogen → trypsin. Without enteropeptidase, no pancreatic zymogens are activated.
Exam Traps Summary
  • Sympathetic → dry mouth (viscous, mucin-rich saliva, less total volume); parasympathetic → watery saliva (more volume).
  • Epiglottis covers the glottis (trachea opening) — not the esophagus — during swallowing.
  • Pepsinogen activates at pH < 5; pepsin has optimal activity at pH ~2 — these are two different pH thresholds.
  • Three gastric acid stimuli (histamine, ACh, gastrin) are synergistic — blocking one still reduces total acid substantially.
  • Enteropeptidase is an intestinal enzyme, not a pancreatic enzyme — it initiates the zymogen activation cascade.
  • Bile salts emulsify fats but do NOT digest them — digestion is by lipase. Emulsification just increases surface area for lipase.
  • Gallbladder stores and concentrates bile — it does not produce bile (hepatocytes produce bile).
Top things to memorize
  1. Saliva: 1.5 L/day; functions — lubrication, dental protection (defensins, alkaline pH), amylase for starch
  2. Amylase products: starch → maltose (2-glucose) + maltotriose (3-glucose) + oligosaccharides (>3)
  3. Swallowing sequence: tongue pushes bolus → epiglottis covers trachea → upper esophageal sphincter relaxes → peristalsis
  4. GERD = lower esophageal sphincter failure; 10–20% Western populations; cimetidine (H₂ blocker) treats it
  5. 5 gastric gland cell types: mucous surface (mucus+HCO₃⁻), mucous neck (mucus), parietal (HCl+intrinsic factor), chief (pepsinogen+gastric lipase), G cell (gastrin)
  6. Parietal cell HCl mechanism: H⁺/K⁺ ATPase (pump) + Cl⁻/HCO₃⁻ exchanger + Cl⁻ channel
  7. 3 gastric acid stimuli: histamine (ECL cells, paracrine), ACh (vagus, neurotransmitter), gastrin (G cells, endocrine) — all synergistic
  8. H. pylori: ~50% world population; urease neutralizes acid; causes gastric ulcers
  9. Pancreatic zymogens activated by enteropeptidase → trypsin → cascade
  10. Bile salts: made from cholesterol; amphipathic; emulsify fats + solubilize cholesterol (prevent gallstones)
  11. Bilirubin (hemoglobin breakdown) → gives bile yellow-green color; excess → jaundice
