## Slide 7

The Function Argument

7

The highest good of human beings concerns their purpose or function as human beings.
The purpose or function of human beings concerns what is distinctive of such beings.
Rationality is distinctive of humans and hence its exercise is central in understanding the function of human beings.
The highest good of human beings involves the rational exercise of the soul (mind).
The good of a thing is that thing performing its function well, which, for humans, involves rational activity guided by virtue.
The highest good of human beings is a life of rational activity of the soul guided by virtue.

## Slide 13

Unity of Virtues

13

Suppose a person is on a military campaign where food and water must be rationed. 
Justice requires that she take only her fair share of supplies. Unless this person is also temperate, she will be disposed to unjustly take more than her fair share. Thus, being a truly just person requires that one also be temperate.

## Slide 15

Virtue Ethics

15

An action A in C is obligatory iff any ideally virtuous agent would not fail to do A in C. 
An action A in C is wrong iff no ideally virtuous agent would perform A in C. 
An action A in C is optional iff some ideally virtuous agent might or might not perform A in C.

## Slide 17

Evaluation

17

Imagine a mendacious person who is a compulsive liar, even about trivial matters that cause little or no harm to others. He has the vice of dishonesty. At some point in life, he becomes convinced that he ought to change because he comes to see mendacity as a vice and honesty as a trait one ought to have. But to bring about this personal change, he cannot simply say to himself with conviction, “ok, from now on I won’t tell lies”; he knows better. What he must do is engage in a regimen that involves taking concrete steps such as writing down lies that he tells and reflecting on their true significance, rewarding himself at the end of those days in which he tells no lies, and other such tactics. But none of these concrete activities are characteristic of what an ideally virtuous agent would engage in.


## Slide 18

An action A performed in circumstance C is obligatory iff any ideally virtuous agent would advise one to not fail to do A in C.
Another counterexample: 
Consider an obstinate person who is determined to do just the opposite of what anyone advises him to do. An ideally virtuous person knowing this might strategically advise this person to not do. A believing that this (false) advice will prompt him to do A. So, what any ideally virtuous agent would advise this person to do is not what he ought to do.

## Slide 26

Care Ethics and Moral Theory

26

Two care ethics principles:
Dependency principle. When an important interest is unfulfilled and you’re capable of fulfilling that interest, and fulfilling the interest will not be too costly, then you have a responsibility to fulfill the interest. (Collins 2015, chapter 6)
“Important interest” includes material as well as non-material needs.


## Slide 27

Two care ethics principles:
Principle of subsidiary. We should shift the actual delivery of care whenever possible to the most local and personal levels. We should care for others whenever possible by enabling them to care for themselves. (Engster 2007, 58)
The application of these principles must be guided by emotional attunement.
