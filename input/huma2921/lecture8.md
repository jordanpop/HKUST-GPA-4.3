## Slide 6

Supreme Principle of Morality

6

Kant distinguishes two sorts of ends: 
Ends that we adopt based on our desires 
They acquire worth or value for us by being objects of our choice. 
Ends in themselves
According to Kant, there is such an end: “Rational nature exists as an end in itself” (G, 4:428–29/79).


## Slide 7

7

“Autonomy is . . . the ground of the dignity of human nature and of every rational nature” (G, 4:436/85).
“The ground of the possibility of categorical imperatives is this: that they refer to no other property of choice (by which some purpose can be ascribed to it) than simply to its freedom” (MM, 6:222/377).


## Slide 8

Formula of Humanity

8

If something has unconditional value, then there is an unconditional requirement to respect it—the kind of response that something with such worth calls for. 
Anything with the status of an end in itself has unconditional value.
Our nature as autonomous agents makes us ends in ourselves.
Thus, there is a basic unconditional requirement to respect the autonomy of agents.


## Slide 9

Formula of Humanity

9

Formula of Humanity (FH):
“So act that you use humanity, in your own person as well as in the person of any other, always at the same time as an end, never merely as a means.” (G, 4:429/80) 
Negative part: Prohibits treating persons merely as a means to one’s ends
Positive part: Requires the promotion of certain ends

## Slide 19

Lizbeth’s lie. Lizbeth is hiding an acquaintance, Tom, who is wrongly believed by some murderous gang members to have ratted out one of their gang to the police. A gang member shows up at Lizbeth’s front door, asking her for the whereabouts of Tom. Telling the truth would surely lead to the murder of Tom, and so would (let us suppose) just refusing to answer. ## Slide 20

An action A in C is obligatory iff failing to perform A in C would fail to respect humanity by treating oneself or others merely as means to some non-moral end. 
An action A in C is wrong iff performing A in C would fail to respect humanity by treating oneself or others merely as means to some non-moral end. 
An action A in C is optional iff neither performing A in C nor omitting A in C fails to respect humanity.

## Slide 21

Formula of Universal Law

21

“Act only according to that maxim through which you can at the same time will that it become a universal law” (G, 4:421/73). 
FH: a fundamental criterion of right action 
FUL: a decision procedure to guide moral deliberation
Maxim: “a subjective principle of volition” (G, 4:402n/56) on which an agent acts whenever she acts intentionally.


## Slide 22

General semantic form of maxim:

Maxim of commission:
	I will keep my promise 	whenever ________. 
Maxim of omission:
	I will omit to keep my promise 	whenever ________.

## Slide 23

Formula of the Law of Nature

23

“So act as if the maxim of your action were to become by your will a universal law of nature” (G, 4:421/73).
FUL/FLN gives what is commonly called “the universalizability test.”
To say that a maxim is universalizable is to say that it can be consistently willed as a law of nature.


## Slide 24

An action A in C is obligatory iff one cannot universalize the maxim of omission associated with A in C. 
An action A in C is wrong iff one cannot universalize the maxim of commission associated with A in C. 
An action A in C is optional iff one can universalize the maxims of commission and omission associated with A.

## Slide 25

The False-Promising Example

25

“Imagine someone who finds himself urged by need to borrow money. He well knows that he will not be able to repay it but sees also that nothing will be lent him unless he promises to repay it within a determinate time. He would like to make such a promise, but he still has enough conscience to ask himself, is it not forbidden and contrary to duty to help oneself out of need in such a way?” (G, 4:422/74)


## Slide 26

Consider a maxim of selfishness: 
I will refrain from helping those in need whenever I can help, even though I am well off. 
It seems to be universalizable without landing us into a contradiction. 
However, it fails FLN by conflicting with a maxim of rationality:
I will to be helped whenever I am in need and others who are well off can help me.
